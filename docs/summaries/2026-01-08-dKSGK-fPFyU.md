# 與Cursor的爐邊對談：AI編碼助理的未來

## 重點摘要
本次對談探討了AI編碼工具的快速演進，從基本的自動完成演變為能在整個程式碼庫中運作的自主代理人。Cursor的工程主管Jason強調，目標是透過提升抽象層次和改善開發者體驗來增強頂尖工程師的能力，而非取代他們。

## 故事大綱
- **開場**：LangChain的共同創辦人Harrison介紹了來自Cursor的Jason。Jason簡述了Cursor作為一個AI編碼工具，並為接下來關於編碼代理人的討論揭開序幕。
- **中段**：Jason闡述了AI編碼的演進，從單行輔助發展到跨檔案的代理人。他介紹了Cursor中的不同模式（如`plan`、`debug`），並強調IDE作為整合開發週期的核心地位。他分享了Cursor快速迭代的文化，這種文化由內部「狗食文化」（dogfooding）驅動，並透露了即將推出的「視覺化編輯器」等新功能。
- **結尾**：對談進入觀眾問答環節，涵蓋了非工程師使用Cursor、智慧合約驗證、代理人的記憶體管理以及Cursor自研模型「Composer」背後的策略。Jason總結道，最大的機會在於讓代理人更智能，並具備自我驗證的能力。

## 關鍵見解
1.  **抽象層次的提升**：編碼代理人的使用者體驗正從逐行輔助，演變為能處理整個程式碼庫的異步代理人，開發者像審查PR一樣審查其變更。
2.  **沒有萬用的使用方式**：不存在所謂「正確」的使用方法。不同的工程師有不同的工作流程，從重度依賴自動完成到完全依賴代理人驅動開發。
3.  **IDE仍是核心**：IDE因其整合了從規劃、編碼、除錯到PR的整個開發流程而至關重要。UI的基礎元件沒變，但其組合方式正為適應代理人工作流而改變。
4.  **「狗食文化」驅動創新**：Cursor的產品開發深受內部團隊需求影響。他們打造自己想用的功能來改善日常工作流程，例如`debug mode`和視覺化編輯器。
5.  **賦能頂尖開發者**：AI的目標不是降低門檻讓所有人都能寫程式，而是「提高天花板」，讓最優秀的工程師能完成更複雜的工作。

## 精彩時刻
- **對「提示詞大師」的看法**：Jason提到：「我常在Twitter上看到有人說『這是最佳使用方式』還附上技巧。我常覺得那是瞎掰的...我們自己就不用那種超長的提示詞。」
- **「狗食文化」的趣聞**：一位工程師改了一個他認為沒人會用的快捷鍵，結果秒收到三位同事的抱怨，這突顯了他們極致的內部產品使用文化。
- **新功能揭露**：即將推出一個「視覺化編輯器」，允許使用者在瀏覽器中直接點擊UI元件並用提示詞進行修改，無縫連接設計與程式碼。
- **為誰而建**：當被問及目標用戶時，Jason回答：「我們優化的主要目標是：誰是世界上最頂尖的工程師？我們如何讓他們變得更強大？」

# Fireside Chat with Cursor: The Future of AI Coding Agents

## TL;DR
This chat explores the rapid evolution of AI coding tools, from basic autocompletion to autonomous agents that operate across an entire codebase. Jason, Head of Engineering at Cursor, emphasizes that the goal is to augment the best engineers by raising the level of abstraction and improving the developer experience, not to replace them.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning**: Harrison, co-founder of Lang Chain, introduces Jason from Cursor. Jason explains that Cursor is an AI coding tool, setting the stage for a discussion on coding agents.
- **Middle**: Jason details the evolution of AI coding from per-line assistance to multi-file agents. He discusses different modes in Cursor (e.g., `plan`, `debug`), the importance of the IDE as the central hub for the development lifecycle, and Cursor's culture of rapid iteration driven by internal "dogfooding." He also reveals upcoming features like a "visual editor."
- **End**: The conversation moves to an audience Q&A, covering topics like Cursor's use by non-engineers, smart contract verification, agent memory/context, and the strategy behind Cursor's custom model, "Composer." Jason concludes that the biggest opportunity lies in making agents more intelligent and capable of self-verification.

## Key Insights
1.  **Rising Abstraction Levels**: The UX for coding agents is evolving from line-by-line assistance to asynchronous, full-codebase agents whose changes are reviewed by developers like a pull request.
2.  **No One-Size-Fits-All Workflow**: There is no single "right way" to use an agent. Different engineers adopt different patterns, from being heavy on tab-completion to being "fully agentic."
3.  **The IDE Remains Crucial**: The IDE is vital because it integrates the entire development lifecycle, from planning and coding to debugging and opening a PR. The fundamental UI components haven't changed, but their arrangement is adapting to agent-based workflows.
4.  **Dogfooding Drives Innovation**: Cursor's product development is heavily guided by what their own team needs. They build features they want (like `debug mode` and a visual editor) to improve their own daily workflows.
5.  **Empowering the Best**: The goal of AI is not to lower the floor so anyone can code, but to "raise the ceiling" for what the best engineers in the world can accomplish.

## Notable Moments
- **On "Prompt Wizards"**: Jason said, "I see on Twitter all the time... when people are like 'this is the best way to use it and have like tips.' Often I think that's kind of made up... we don't have these crazy long prompts."
- **The "Dogfooding" Anecdote**: An engineer changed what he thought was an obscure key binding, only to get instant complaints from three colleagues, highlighting the team's intense internal use of their own product.
- **New Feature Reveal**: A "visual editor" is coming, allowing users to click on UI elements in a browser and prompt changes directly, bridging the gap between design and code.
- **Who They Build For**: When asked about their target user, Jason replied, "The main objective that we're optimizing for is: who's the best engineer in the world? How can we make them more capable?"
