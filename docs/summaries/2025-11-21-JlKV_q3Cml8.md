# 世紀帝國2：高手1v3，但決定先砍光對手所有的樹

## 重點摘要
一名職業玩家在一場與三名初學者的1v3對戰中，放棄了快速取勝的機會。他轉而執行一個獨特的「支線任務」：用攻城武器系統性地砍伐對手地圖上的所有樹木，最終透過徹底的「木材控制」策略，癱瘓了對手的經濟並贏得了一場極具娛樂性的勝利。

## 故事大綱
- **開場**：職業玩家Hera在一張亞馬遜隧道的1v3地圖上對抗三位ELO（積分）低得多的玩家。他隨機選到了契丹文明，並決定採用快速封建時代的開局，用斥侯進行早期騷擾。
- **中段**：在成功地用斥侯騷擾並擾亂對手後，Hera認為如果直接結束比賽會很無趣。因此，他給自己設定了一個「支線任務」：不直接攻擊，而是使用他的攻城單位（回回炮）去砍光對手半場的所有樹木。他將大量時間投入到這個他認為非常療癒且有趣的過程中，同時也給了對手們發展和集結軍隊的時間。
- **結尾**：對手最終集結了一支龐大的軍隊並發動了猛烈的攻擊，迫使Hera必須認真防守。此時，他長時間執行的「木材控制」策略開始顯現威力：對手因為沒有木材而無法重建農田、補充兵力。Hera成功守住攻勢，並用石牆將最後的樹木圍起來，對手經濟崩潰，最終只能投降。

## 關鍵見解
1.  **木材是終極資源**：在長局遊戲中，木材比黃金更為關鍵。完全剝奪對手的木材可以癱瘓其整個經濟體系，從食物生產到軍事單位補充都會停擺。
2.  **娛樂性大於效率**：在實力懸殊的對戰中，為自己設定一個非傳統的挑戰或「支線任務」，可以讓遊戲對玩家和觀眾來說都更加有趣和難忘。
3.  **後期遊戲的威脅**：永遠不要低估你的對手，即使是初學者。如果給他們足夠的時間發展到後期，他們仍然可以集結出具有威脅性的力量。
4.  **創意玩法的重要性**：跳出常規的戰術框架，嘗試創新的策略不僅充滿樂趣，甚至可能讓你發現新的技巧，並為遊戲帶來全新的體驗。
5.  **享受遊戲過程**：Hera的結論是，偶爾放下追求100%勝率的壓力，純粹為了好玩而玩，嘗試一些瘋狂的點子，正是遊戲的樂趣所在。

## 精彩時刻
- **策略轉折點**：Hera在早期取得優勢後，決定不結束遊戲，反而開始執行砍光所有樹木的瘋狂計畫。
- **伐木的樂趣**：他一邊用攻城武器 methodical地清除森林，一邊發表如「鮑伯·魯斯作畫」般輕鬆療癒的旁白，對這個過程樂在其中。
- **戰況緊張時刻**：當對手發動大規模總攻時，Hera被迫「坐直身體」認真應對，遊戲一度變得非常緊張。
- **決定性的勝利畫面**：遊戲結束時，對手的村民因無樹可砍而四處遊蕩，經濟完全停滯，完美印證了他策略的成功。
- **幽默的評論**：他開玩笑地指控對手作弊，並說他想「看到對手因缺木而受苦的樣子」，為整個過程增添了許多趣味。

# 1v3 vs. Beginners, But I Decided to Cut Down Every Tree on Their Map

## TL;DR
A pro player in a 1v3 match against beginners forgoes a quick victory, instead undertaking a unique "side quest" to systematically chop down all his opponents' wood with siege units. This unconventional "wood control" strategy ultimately cripples their economy and secures a unique, entertaining win.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning**: Pro player Hera starts a 1v3 match on Amazon Tunnel against three much lower-ranked players. He gets the Kitan civilization and decides on a Fast Feudal opening to apply early pressure with scouts.
- **Middle**: After successfully harassing and disrupting his opponents, Hera feels a straightforward win would be boring. He creates a "side quest" for himself: instead of attacking directly, he uses his siege units (Rocket Carts) to methodically clear every single tree on the opponents' half of the map. He spends a significant amount of time on this process, which he finds relaxing and fun, while allowing his opponents time to build up their forces.
- **End**: The opponents eventually amass a large army and launch a powerful, serious attack, forcing Hera to defend properly. His long-term "wood control" strategy then pays off decisively: the opponents run out of wood and cannot replenish their farms or army. Hera successfully defends the push, walls off the last remaining trees, and wins as their economies collapse.

## Key Insights
1.  **Wood is the Ultimate Resource**: In a long game, wood can be more critical than gold. Complete wood denial can cripple an opponent's entire economy, from food production to military reinforcement.
2.  **Entertainment Over Efficiency**: In lopsided matches, creating self-imposed challenges or "side quests" can lead to more memorable and enjoyable content for both the player and the audience than a simple stomp.
3.  **The Late-Game Threat**: Never underestimate opponents, even beginners, if they are given enough time to reach the late game. They can still build a threatening force.
4.  **The Value of Creative Play**: Stepping away from standard meta strategies is not only fun but can also lead to the discovery of new techniques and provide a fresh perspective on the game.
5.  **Enjoying the Process**: Hera concludes that sometimes stepping back from playing 100% optimally and just having fun with a crazy idea is what makes the game enjoyable.

## Notable Moments
- **The Strategic Pivot**: After gaining an early advantage, Hera's decision to not end the game and instead begin his wild plan to cut down all the trees.
- **The Joy of Chopping**: His commentary, describing the methodical forest clearing as a "Bob Ross episode," showing his genuine enjoyment of the process.
- **The Tense Climax**: The moment the opponents' massive push becomes a real threat, forcing Hera to "sit up" and play seriously.
- **The Decisive Victory Screen**: At the end of the game, the opponents' villagers are idle with no wood to chop, perfectly illustrating the success of his bizarre strategy.
- **Humorous Commentary**: His jokes about accusing a beginner of cheating and wanting to "see them suffer" from the lack of wood added a lot of personality to the game.
