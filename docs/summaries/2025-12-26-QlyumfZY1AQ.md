# 2025年市場回顧與2026年展望：AI、人性與投資策略

## 重點摘要
本影片回顧了2025年由關稅衝擊和AI熱潮驅動的極端波動市場，並展望2026年。核心焦點將從AI基礎建設轉向實際獲利能力，各大投行看法分歧，但最終的投資哲學回歸到不變的人性，強調在市場恐慌時保持定力的重要性。

## 故事大綱
- 開場：節目回顧2025年美股走勢，雖然在平安夜創下歷史新高，但全年經歷了上半年因川普關稅引發的熊市，以及下半年由AI帶動的牛市，波動極為劇烈。
- 中段：深入分析2025年的幾大市場現象：輝達市值超越蘋果、美元走弱使海外市場（如台股）表現優於美股、黃金成為年度最佳資產（漲71%）、以及比特幣的劇烈震盪。接著，報告引用大摩、高盛、美銀對2026年的展望，探討AI如何從投入轉為獲利、生產力是否爆發，以及估值過高的疑慮。演講者進一步探討社會政治變化，以及OpenAI在與Google等全能巨頭競爭中面臨的挑戰。
- 結尾：結論是投資的本質是研究人性。講者以Google熱搜關鍵字的變化和有趣的戀愛選擇比喻，說明人性的恆定與策略中的「放棄」。最後推薦《蘋果在中國》一書，作為理解產業競爭與全球策略的案例，並總結2026年的市場觀察重點。

## 關鍵見解
- **波動是常態，人性是關鍵：** 2025年市場從高點回落15%再創新高，證明了「死在波動」是投資者最大的風險。理解人性中的恐懼與貪婪，並在市場低迷時保持信心，是長期獲利的基礎。
- **AI的下半場：從基建到獲利：** 市場對AI的關注正從「投入多少錢蓋基建」轉向「誰能真正將AI轉化為利潤」。2026年將是檢視哪些公司能推出殺手級應用、掌握生態系並實現商業價值的一年。
- **美元走弱改變全球投資格局：** 2025年美元貶值近10%，使得以美元計價的海外資產（如台韓股市）報酬率極佳。這為2026年帶來一個核心抉擇：投資於貨幣可能升值的新興市場，還是堅守擁有頂尖科技的美國企業？
- **巨頭的生存戰略—學會放棄：** OpenAI雖強，但在面對Google這種擁有模型、數據、硬體、通路和現金流的「全能巨獸」時，若想在所有戰線競爭將非常困難。成功的策略可能需要像戀愛一樣，學會「放棄」某些領域，專注於自身的核心優勢賽道。
- **萬變不離其宗的投資哲學：** 演講者總結，研究投資的終點是研究人性。從金融、經濟、政治、歷史到哲學，最終會發現市場規律源於不變的人性。無論每年擔心的黑天鵝是什麼（貿易戰、疫情、通膨、AI泡沫），優質資產長期趨勢向上。

## 精彩時刻
- **投資的修練之路：** 「交易剛開始時，都覺得自己在研究金融；深入後發現是在研究經濟；再深入是在研究政治；走下去是研究歷史，然後變哲學；最終發現，原來是研究人性。」
- **用戀愛比喻商業策略：** 建議OpenAI應「學會放棄」，就像一個條件好的追求者為你放棄更多選擇，才代表真心。OpenAI不應與Google全面開戰，而應專注特定領域，才能勝出，因為「沒有放棄就沒有真愛」。
- **從Google搜尋看人性：** 比較2024和2025年台灣熱搜榜，發現前幾名總是「翻譯、天氣」等基本需求，後面則是「NBA、A片、餐廳、539」等娛樂。結論：人性不變，只是娛樂方式稍微改變了。
- **牛熊之辯的幽默總結：** 對於2025年市場到底是牛是熊，講者戲稱：「到底是牛中帶熊，還是熊中帶牛？是樓中帶缸，還是缸中帶樓？還是缸樓并計，牛熊香蕉，軟硬監獅，背歡禮。」

# 2025 Market Review & 2026 Outlook: AI, Human Nature, and Investment Strategy

## TL;DR
This video recaps the extremely volatile 2025 market, driven by tariff shocks and an AI boom, while looking ahead to 2026. The core focus is shifting from AI infrastructure to actual profitability, with major investment banks offering divergent views, but the ultimate investment philosophy circles back to unchanging human nature and the importance of holding steady during market panic.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning:** The show opens by reviewing the 2025 US stock market. Despite hitting all-time highs on Christmas Eve, the year was marked by a bear market in the first half caused by Trump's tariffs, followed by an AI-led bull market in the second half, resulting in extreme volatility.
- **Middle:** The analysis delves into several key 2025 market phenomena: Nvidia surpassing Apple in market cap, a weakening US Dollar making overseas markets (like Taiwan) outperform the US, gold becoming the year's best-performing asset (+71%), and the wild swings of Bitcoin. It then presents the 2026 outlooks from Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs, and Bank of America, debating how AI will transition from investment to profit, whether productivity will surge, and concerns about high valuations. The speaker also discusses socio-political shifts and the competitive challenge OpenAI faces against all-in-one giants like Google.
- **End:** The host concludes that the essence of investing is studying human nature. He uses analogies from Google search trends and quirky relationship advice to illustrate the constancy of human behavior and the strategy of "giving things up." Finally, he recommends the book "Apple in China" as a case study for industrial competition and global strategy, summarizing the key market themes for 2026.

## Key Insights
- **Volatility is the Norm, Psychology is Key:** The 2025 market, which fell 15% from its highs before roaring back, proves that "dying in the volatility" is the greatest risk for investors. Understanding human emotions of fear and greed and maintaining conviction during downturns is fundamental to long-term success.
- **AI's Second Act: From Infrastructure to Profit:** The market's focus on AI is shifting from "how much is being spent on infrastructure" to "who can actually turn AI into profit." 2026 will be the year to watch which companies can launch killer apps, control the ecosystem, and achieve commercial success.
- **The Weak Dollar Changed the Game:** The US dollar's nearly 10% decline in 2025 dramatically boosted returns for dollar-based investors in overseas assets (like stocks in Taiwan and South Korea). This creates a key strategic choice for 2026: invest in emerging markets for currency appreciation or stick with US companies for their technological dominance.
- **A Giant's Survival Strategy—Learn to Give Up:** While OpenAI is a leader, it faces immense pressure from "all-in-one giants" like Google, which possess the model, data, hardware, distribution, and cash flow. A winning strategy may require "giving up" on some fronts to focus on core strengths, much like in a relationship.
- **An Unchanging Investment Philosophy:** The speaker concludes that the study of investing ultimately becomes the study of human nature. No matter the "black swan" event of the year (trade wars, pandemics, inflation, AI bubbles), the long-term trend for quality assets is upward because human nature is constant.

## Notable Moments
- **The Path of an Investor's Study:** "When you start trading, you think you're studying finance. Go deeper, and you find you're studying economics. Deeper still, it's politics. Then it's history, then philosophy. Finally, you realize you're studying human nature."
- **Business Strategy as Dating Advice:** He advises OpenAI to "learn to give up," arguing that, like a desirable partner who forgoes other options for you, true commitment is shown through sacrifice. OpenAI can't win by fighting Google on every front and must focus to succeed, because "without sacrifice, there is no true love."
- **Human Nature via Google Searches:** Comparing 2024 and 2025 search trends in Taiwan, he notes the top queries are always basic needs ("translate," "weather"), followed by entertainment ("NBA," "adult films," "restaurants," "lottery"). The conclusion: human nature is constant, even if the preferred form of entertainment changes slightly.
- **A Humorous Take on the Bull/Bear Debate:** When describing the 2025 market, the speaker humorously asks: "Was it a bull within a bear, or a bear within a bull? Or a mix of both, a 'bull-bear-na,' hard and soft, joy and sorrow all at once."
