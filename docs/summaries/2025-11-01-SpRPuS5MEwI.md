# M5 Vision Pro 深度評測：一次「幾乎無感」的升級

## 重點摘要
新款 M5 Vision Pro 的體驗升級微乎其微，其流暢度與反應速度的提升主要歸功於新的 VisionOS 系統，而非 M5 晶片。對於絕大多數用戶而言，購買一台二手的 M2 Vision Pro 並升級至最新系統，是遠比花大錢買 M5 版本更具性價比的選擇。

## 故事大綱
- 開場：評測者初次戴上新款 M5 Vision Pro，對其流暢的動畫、精準的手勢辨識和更清晰的畫面感到驚艷，一度以為 M5 晶片帶來了革命性的改變。
- 中段：為了驗證，他將舊款 M2 Vision Pro 升級到最新的 VisionOS 26 系統，結果發現 M2 機型也變得同樣流暢好用，從而揭示了「體驗升級來自軟體，而非硬體」的真相。評測者接著詳細分析 M5 版的幾大升級點（M5 晶片、120Hz 螢幕），發現因缺乏軟體支援，這些強大的硬體規格在日常使用中幾乎無法被感知。唯一真正實用的升級是更舒適的新款雙環編織頭帶。
- 結尾：評測者總結，M5 Vision Pro 目前不值得用原價購買。他轉而介紹了 Vision Pro 在 2025 年的幾大用途：用來向親友展示的「炫技」App、觀看個人下載影片的「私人大螢幕」、以及作為 Mac 的「虛擬顯示器」。他強調其辦公和遊戲生態依然極度匱乏，最終建議想嚐鮮的用戶購買二手 M2 版本即可。

## 關鍵見解
1.  **軟體優化大於硬體升級**：VisionOS 系統的更新對使用者體驗的提升，遠比從 M2 換到 M5 晶片更為顯著。
2.  **硬體性能過剩**：M5 晶片近乎翻倍的性能和 120Hz 的螢幕更新率，因缺乏對應的應用程式和場景支援，成為了普通用戶幾乎無法利用的「紙上參數」。
3.  **最佳用途是個人影音與 Mac 擴展**：Vision Pro 目前最成熟的應用是作為個人影音設備（特別是搭配 Infuse 播放本地檔案）和 Mac 的外接虛擬螢幕，而非獨立的生產力或遊戲平台。
4.  **二手 M2 機型是CP值首選**：對於想體驗空間計算的消費者，購買價格大幅下降的二手 M2 Vision Pro 並升級系統，能以遠低於 M5 新機的成本獲得幾乎相同的核心體驗。
5.  **生態系統仍是短版**：儘管硬體先進，但 Vision Pro 在主流應用（如串流影音、辦公軟體）和遊戲內容上依然極度匱乏，限制了其成為大眾化設備的潛力。

## 精彩時刻
- **真相大白時刻**：當評測者升級舊款 M2 主機後，發現流暢體驗與 M5 版本無異，意識到自己差點被「硬體升級」的表象所欺騙。
- **對 60Hz 的吐槽**：對於 Mac 虛擬螢幕能支援超高解析度，卻只能以 60Hz 更新率串流，評測者直言蘋果給出的頻寬不足的理由「簡直是笑話」。
- **「炫技 App」的定義**：形容那些效果驚人、但「你大概只會打開一次，再也不會打開第二次，但第一次的印象就足以讓你回味好幾年」的應用。
- **航空迷的夢幻逸品**：Voyager App 能以 3D 方式即時呈現全球航班的飛行姿態，被譽為「真正的上帝視角」，是航空迷的必備應用。
- **最終結論**：M5 Vision Pro 是一個「酷，但也就只是酷」的願景，花一萬多人民幣嚐鮮尚可，但花三萬買全新的 M5 版本則完全沒有必要。

# M5 Vision Pro In-depth Review: An "Almost Imperceptible" Upgrade

## TL;DR
The new M5 Vision Pro offers a minimal experiential upgrade, with its improved smoothness and responsiveness coming primarily from the new VisionOS software, not the M5 chip. For the vast majority of users, buying a second-hand M2 Vision Pro and updating it to the latest OS is a far more cost-effective choice than splurging on the M5 model.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning:** The reviewer first puts on the new M5 Vision Pro and is impressed by its smooth animations, precise gesture recognition, and clearer display, initially believing the M5 chip delivered a revolutionary leap.
- **Middle:** To verify this, he updates his old M2 Vision Pro to the latest VisionOS 26 system. He discovers that the M2 model becomes just as smooth and easy to use, revealing the truth: the user experience improvements come from software, not hardware. The reviewer then breaks down the M5's major upgrades (M5 chip, 120Hz screen), finding that due to a lack of software support, these powerful hardware specs are nearly imperceptible in daily use. The only truly practical upgrade is the new, more comfortable dual-loop braided headband.
- **End:** The reviewer concludes that the M5 Vision Pro is not worth its high price at this time. He then explores the primary use cases for Vision Pro in 2025: "show-off" apps to impress friends and family, a "personal big screen" for watching downloaded movies, and a "virtual display" for a Mac. He emphasizes that its productivity and gaming ecosystems are still severely lacking and ultimately recommends that users who want to try it out should just buy a second-hand M2 version.

## Key Insights
1.  **Software Optimization Outweighs Hardware Upgrades:** The VisionOS system update provides a more significant improvement to the user experience than the hardware jump from the M2 to the M5 chip.
2.  **Hardware Power is Overkill:** The M5 chip's nearly doubled performance and the 120Hz screen refresh rate are "paper specs" that average users can't leverage due to a lack of supporting applications and scenarios.
3.  **Best Use Cases are Personal Media & Mac Extension:** The most mature applications for Vision Pro currently are as a personal media device (especially with Infuse for local files) and as an external virtual monitor for a Mac, not as a standalone productivity or gaming platform.
4.  **A Second-Hand M2 is the Value Choice:** For consumers wanting to experience spatial computing, buying a heavily discounted second-hand M2 Vision Pro and updating its OS provides nearly the same core experience for a fraction of the cost of a new M5 model.
5.  **The Ecosystem Remains the Weakest Link:** Despite its advanced hardware, the Vision Pro is still extremely deficient in mainstream applications (like streaming services, office software) and gaming content, limiting its potential as a mass-market device.

## Notable Moments
- **The "Mystery Solved" Moment:** When the reviewer upgrades his old M2 unit and finds the smooth experience is identical to the M5 version, realizing he was almost fooled by the illusion of a hardware upgrade.
- **The 60Hz Rant:** Regarding the Mac virtual display supporting massive resolutions but being limited to a 60Hz stream, the reviewer calls Apple's excuse of insufficient bandwidth "a joke."
- **The Definition of "Show-off Apps":** Describing apps that are so amazing "you'll probably only open them once and never again, but that first impression is enough to be memorable for years."
- **Aviation Enthusiast's Dream App:** The Voyager app, which visualizes global flights in 3D with real-time data, is hailed as a "true God's-eye view" and a must-have for aviation fans.
- **The Final Verdict:** The M5 Vision Pro is a "cool, but ultimately just cool, vision." It might be worth spending over 10,000 RMB (~$1,500) for a novel experience, but spending 30,000 RMB (~$4,500) on a brand new M5 is completely unnecessary.
