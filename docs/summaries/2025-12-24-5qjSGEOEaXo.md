# 延遲多少才算太多？

## 重點摘要
一項針對不同技術水平玩家的實驗顯示，對高手而言，即使是3毫秒(ms)的延遲也會明顯降低表現。雖然巨大的技術差距可以克服高延遲劣勢，但對於競技玩家來說，將延遲降至最低至關重要，而螢幕設定往往是最大的延遲來源。

## 故事大綱
- **開場：** Linus Tech Tips 團隊設置了一項實驗，找來四位不同等級的玩家（從休閒玩家到職業選手），使用特製設備精確增加延遲（0-100ms），旨在量化輸入延遲對遊戲表現的具體影響。
- **中段：** 玩家們首先進行 AimLabs 射擊訓練。數據顯示，即使是微小的延遲也會影響表現，而玩家約在35-50ms時才開始主觀感覺到「卡頓」。接著，他們進行了1v1對戰，讓技術較差的 Linus 對上高手，並逐步增加高手的延遲。結果顯示，需要非常大的延遲（75ms+）才能稍微拉近業餘和職業玩家的差距，凸顯了原始技術和「意識」的重要性遠超設備優勢。
- **結尾：** 最終數據分析揭曉，對於頂尖玩家，每增加3ms的延遲，遊戲表現就會穩定線性下降。結論是：雖然技術是王道，但對想要求勝的玩家來說，任何程度的延遲都有害無益。硬體確實有影響，特別是螢幕模式，它可能是系統中最大的延遲來源。

## 關鍵見解
1.  **高手對延遲極其敏感：** 對於頂尖玩家，不存在「感覺不到就沒影響」的延遲閾值。數據證明，低至3ms的延遲就會確實地、線性地降低他們的操作表現。
2.  **表現下降先於體感：** 遊戲分數的下降在極低的延遲（約10ms）時就已發生，遠早於大多數玩家能主觀感覺到延遲（約35-50ms）的時刻。
3.  **技術鴻溝難以用延遲彌補：** 一位職業玩家即使被加上75ms的巨量延遲，依然能憑藉其「意識」（對遊戲的直覺和經驗）輕鬆擊敗業餘玩家。技術本身是最大的變數。
4.  **檢查你的螢幕設定：** 報告指出，滑鼠等周邊延遲已極低，但許多玩家忽略了螢幕本身。若螢幕處於「電影模式」而非「遊戲模式」，可能引入高達100ms的延遲，這足以完全改變戰局。
5.  **付費致勝？看人：** 影片的最終結論是，低延遲設備是否為「付費致勝」？答案是：「如果你有那個技術（意識），那它就是。如果你沒有，再好的設備也救不了你。」

## 精彩時刻
- 職業選手 Kaanada 形容高延遲的感覺像是「在泥漿中拖行」。
- 在1v1對戰中，Linus 被輕易擊敗後，被隊友戲稱擁有「負意識」（Negative Awareness）。
- 「你得找到『意識』」，這句話成為職業選手用來形容那種超越操作的、純粹的遊戲直覺的術語。
- 當被問到對今天有什麼驚訝時，一位參與者回答：「驚訝的是，這居然是你們的工作。」

# How Much Latency is TOO MUCH?

## TL;DR
An experiment with gamers of different skill levels reveals that for skilled players, even 3ms of latency measurably degrades performance. While a huge skill gap can overcome a large latency disadvantage, for competitive players, minimizing latency is crucial, with monitors often being the biggest culprit.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning:** The Linus Tech Tips team sets up an experiment with four gamers of varying skill levels (from casual to pro) to quantify how input latency affects gaming performance, using a custom rig to add precise delays from 0-100ms.
- **Middle:** The gamers first play AimLabs, where data shows even small amounts of latency impact scores, long before players "feel" the lag around 35-50ms. They then move to a 1v1 FPS duel, handicapping a pro player with latency against the less-skilled Linus. It takes a massive amount of latency (75ms+) to even the odds slightly, proving that raw skill and "awareness" dominate any equipment advantage.
- **End:** The final data analysis reveals a clear, linear performance drop for skilled players with as little as 3ms of added latency. The conclusion is that while skill is paramount, any amount of latency is detrimental for competitive gamers, and hardware—especially monitor settings—can be the largest source of it.

## Key Insights
1.  **Skill Dictates Sensitivity:** For high-skill gamers, there is no "safe" threshold for latency. As little as 3ms has a measurable, linear negative impact on their performance.
2.  **Performance Drops Before It's "Felt":** Performance degradation begins at very low latency levels (~10ms), well before most players consciously perceive the lag, which they reported feeling at around 35-50ms.
3.  **Skill Trumps (a lot of) Latency:** A highly skilled player can still dominate a less-skilled one even when handicapped with significant latency (75ms+), proving that "game sense" or "awareness" is a massive factor that can't be bought.
4.  **Your Monitor is a Latency Hog:** The video points out that while peripherals like mice are low-latency, monitors are often the biggest source of delay. Using "Film Mode" instead of "Game Mode" can add as much as 100ms.
5.  **Pay to Win? Only if You Have Skill:** The final verdict on whether low-latency gear is "pay to win" is: "Well, if you have the awareness, then yes. If you don't, then there's not much you can do about it."

## Notable Moments
- Kaanada, the pro gamer, describing high latency as feeling like you're "dragging through mud."
- Linus being jokingly told he has "negative awareness" after being consistently outplayed in the 1v1 duel.
- The term "awareness" being coined to describe the innate game sense a pro has, which allows them to overcome disadvantages.
- When asked what surprised him most about the day, a participant replied, "That this is your guys's job."
