# 一個比蘋果原廠更棒的粉絲自製滑鼠

## 重點摘要
一位科技愛好者在展會上展示了他改造的蘋果滑鼠，解決了原廠設計的諸多痛點。他不僅將充電口移至前方，還透過觸覺回饋技術，讓單一按鍵實現了左右鍵的完整功能。

## 故事大綱
- 開場：影片開頭直接展示了一款經過改造的蘋果滑鼠，並宣稱它比蘋果官方的設計還要出色。
- 中段：詳細介紹其創新功能，包括頂部的 USB-C 充電口（解決了原廠滑鼠無法在充電時使用的問題）、一個能透過觸覺回饋模擬左右鍵的單一按鍵，以及一個設計獨特的電源按鈕。
- 結尾：當被問及為何要這麼做時，創作者簡短回答他對「修復舊技術」的熱情。影片最後感謝了臨時贊助商 Dbrand。

## 關鍵見解
- **用戶創新勝過官方**：一個普通愛好者就能解決大公司長期忽略的設計缺陷。
- **設計應以人為本**：將充電口移到前方，這個簡單的改動極大地提升了用戶體驗。
- **簡潔與功能的結合**：透過觸覺回饋技術，單一按鍵也能提供完整且直觀的操作，證明了極簡設計的潛力。
- **熱情是創新的驅動力**：對技術的熱愛是催生傑出作品的核心原因。

# A Fan-Made Apple Mouse That's Better Than Apple's

## TL;DR
A tech enthusiast showcased his custom-modified Apple mouse, fixing major design flaws like the bottom charging port. He implemented a clever single-button system with haptic feedback that successfully emulates both left and right clicks.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning**: The video introduces a custom-made Apple mouse, immediately claiming it's superior to Apple's official version.
- **Middle**: It details the mouse's innovative features: a top-mounted USB-C port (solving the infamous charge-and-use problem), a single button using haptic feedback to function as both left and right-click, and a unique power button.
- **End**: When asked for his motivation, the creator simply states, "I love restoring old technology." The video concludes with a shout-out to Dbrand for stepping in as a sponsor.

## Key Insights
- **User-Driven Innovation**: A single enthusiast can identify and solve design flaws that a major corporation has long ignored.
- **Human-Centered Design**: Moving the charging port to the front is a simple change that dramatically improves the user experience.
- **Simplicity Meets Functionality**: Haptic technology allows a single button to provide full, intuitive functionality, proving the potential of minimalist design.
- **Passion Fuels Creation**: A love for technology is the core driver behind this impressive and practical project.

## Notable Moments
- "This random guy on the show floor made a better Apple mouse than Apple could ever make."
- "USBC charging, not on the bottom."
- When asked why he did it, the creator’s simple reply: "I love restoring old technology."
