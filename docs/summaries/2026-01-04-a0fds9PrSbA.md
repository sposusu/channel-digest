# 《世紀帝國II》菁英神Carry：2打4逆境下的聖騎海戰術

## 重點摘要
一名《世紀帝國II》菁英玩家與朋友組隊，挑戰一場2對4的劣勢對戰。儘管隊友開局陷入困境，但該玩家利用勃根地文明的經濟優勢，成功控制地圖上所有聖物，最終爆發出無人能擋的聖騎士（Paladin）海，逐一擊潰四名對手，贏得勝利。

## 故事大綱
- **開場**：玩家介紹這是一場與「壽星」隊友（Fox）聯手，在阿拉伯地圖上進行的2對4隨機文明對戰。他抽到勃根地，計畫扮演騎兵核心，而隊友的孟加拉則負責弓兵。
- **中段**：玩家開局用斥候騷擾，卻發現隊友 Fox 嚴重落後，升上城堡時代的時間過晚。他沒有選擇直接回防，而是發動毀滅性的反擊，重創一名對手（橘色），使其幾乎出局。接著，他專注於發展經濟，憑藉勃根地聖物能產出食物的特性，收集了地圖上全部八個聖物，建立了巨大的經濟優勢，並順利升級到聖騎士。
- **結尾**：憑藉著源源不絕的聖騎士，玩家化身為遊戲中最強大的力量。他利用聖騎士的高機動性和戰鬥力，對敵方玩家進行多線騷擾與殲滅，逐一瓦解他們的防線。即使敵人集結了克制單位，也無法抵擋聖騎士海的衝擊。最終，四名對手無力回天，打出「GG」投降。遊戲結束後，玩家覆盤了勝利的關鍵，並風趣地「吐槽」了隊友那慢到「令人無法接受」的升級時間。

## 關鍵見解
1.  **聖物控制是致勝關鍵**：對於勃根地文明，早期控制聖物不僅提供黃金，還能帶來食物收入，為爆發強大的軍隊奠定雄厚的經濟基礎。
2.  **反擊優於被動防守**：當隊友受壓時，與其被動防守，不如發動一次果斷的反擊，打擊敵方陣營的其中一名玩家，更能有效扭轉局勢。
3.  **最大化文明優勢**：玩家充分利用了勃根地的經濟科技折扣和強大的騎兵單位（Cavalier/Paladin），在遊戲中後期創造出無法阻擋的雪球效應。
4.  **吸收壓力也是一種貢獻**：即使隊友在經濟和軍事上落後，但他成功吸引了多名敵人的火力，為主力玩家（Carry）創造了寶貴的發展時間與空間。
5.  **逐一擊破的戰術**：在多人對戰中，與其分散兵力，不如集中優勢力量，優先癱瘓或擊潰一名對手，以形成局部的人數優勢。

## 精彩時刻
- 玩家評論自己拒絕送隊友黃金時的玩笑話：「我是實況主，我只收斗內，不給斗內……我可以給食物和木材，但黃金不行，我要留著出聖騎士。」
- 當他反擊並摧毀橘色玩家的基地時，他評論說，當隊友被打時，你不能只是掛機（AFK），必須有所作為。
- 遊戲結束後，他幽默地批評隊友 Fox 26分鐘才升上城堡時代，稱之為「令人無法接受的時間」，並開玩笑說「難怪他們都去打你」。
- 玩家成功用聖騎士海淹沒對手，展現了「一人軍隊」的強大宰制力。

# Age of Empires II Pro Carry: A 2v4 Paladin Flood

## TL;DR
An expert Age of Empires II player teams up with a friend for a 2 vs. 4 match. Despite his teammate falling behind early, the player leverages the Burgundian civilization's economic power, secures every relic on the map, and unleashes an unstoppable flood of Paladins to dismantle the four-player enemy team for the win.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning**: The narrator introduces a 2v4 match on Arabia with random civilizations, teaming up with his "birthday guy" friend, Fox. The narrator gets Burgundians and plans a cavalry-focused strategy, while his teammate (Bengalis) is expected to play archers.
- **Middle**: After an initial scout rush, the narrator realizes his teammate is struggling and is very late to the Castle Age. Instead of defending, he launches a devastating counter-attack on one opponent (Orange), effectively crippling them. He then focuses on his economy, collecting all eight of the map's relics—which generate food as Burgundians—to build a massive economic lead and tech into his ultimate unit: the Paladin.
- **End**: With a seemingly endless stream of Paladins, the narrator becomes the most powerful force in the game. He uses the Paladins' strength and mobility to raid and eliminate the enemy players one by one. The opposing team, unable to handle the relentless pressure, eventually calls "GG" and resigns. In his recap, the narrator highlights the key strategic decisions and humorously roasts his teammate for his "unacceptable" slow start.

## Key Insights
1.  **Relic Control is a Winning Strategy**: With the Burgundian civ, securing relics early provides a massive economic advantage (in both gold and food), fueling a powerful, late-game army.
2.  **Counter-Attacking is Better Than Passive Defense**: When a teammate is under pressure, a decisive counter-attack on an exposed enemy can be more effective at turning the game's momentum than simply trying to defend.
3.  **Exploit Your Civilization's Strengths**: The player fully utilized the Burgundians' discounted economic upgrades and powerful Cavalier-to-Paladin unit line to create an unstoppable snowball effect.
4.  **Absorbing Pressure is a Form of Contribution**: Even a player who is behind can significantly help by acting as a "sponge," tying up the attention and resources of multiple opponents and creating space for the carry player to boom.
5.  **Isolate and Eliminate**: In a multi-opponent game, focusing superior force to knock out one player at a time is a crucial tactic for creating a numbers advantage.

## Notable Moments
- The narrator's joke about not sending his teammate gold: "I'm the streamer. I receive donations. I don't give donations... I can give food and wood, but no gold. Never gold. I need it for paladin."
- His commentary while counter-attacking the Orange player, noting that when your teammate is getting smashed, you "can't be just AFK at home."
- The post-game roast where he calls his teammate's 26-minute Castle Age time "unacceptable" and jokes, "No wonder they attacked you."
- The sheer dominance of the Paladin flood, showcasing a "one-man army" that single-handedly took over the game.
