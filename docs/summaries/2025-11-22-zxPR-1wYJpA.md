# Why Pro Players Love the Khmer

**Channel:** Hera - Age of Empires 2

Here is a summary of the YouTube video transcript, preserving its storyline and narrative flow.

### 1. TL;DR

The Khmer are an S-tier civilization in Age of Empires 2, not because of their famous late-game elephants, but due to incredibly powerful and subtle early-game economic bonuses. Pros exploit these advantages—like skipping buildings to save resources and having hyper-efficient farms—to create unmatched tempo and defensive resilience, making the Khmer a top-tier pick on open maps.

### 2. Story/Content Flow

**Beginning:** The video opens by contrasting obviously strong civilizations, like the Bohemians, with the Khmer. To a casual player, the Khmer look like a situational, late-game civ reliant on elephants and siege. The host immediately debunks this, stating that pros consider them one of the best civs in the game for reasons that have nothing to do with their elephants.

**Middle:** The core of the video breaks down the four "secret" reasons for the Khmer's dominance:

1.  **Extreme Early Game Savings:** The Khmer don't need prerequisite buildings to advance ages or unlock other buildings. This means they can skip the barracks (saving 175 wood and villager build time) and go straight for a stable or range in the Feudal Age. Additionally, their farms deposit food directly into the stockpile without needing a mill or Town Center, providing a faster, more consistent food income that gives them a crucial tempo advantage in producing units like scouts.

2.  **Unique, Powerful Build Orders:** These savings unlock strategies other civs can't do, like an ultra-fast 16-villager scout rush or a 13-minute Fast Castle. While other civs would be economically crippled by such aggressive timings, the Khmer's farming bonus allows them to sustain a strong economy behind it. (The host then makes a shameless, but funny, plug for his Patreon where he provides guides for these builds).

3.  **Incredible Defense via House Garrisoning:** Khmer villagers can garrison inside houses. This seemingly small bonus is game-changing for defense. Players can "house hop" to save villagers from raids, making it incredibly risky for an opponent to attack, as they might lose army health without securing any villager kills. It allows the Khmer to defend with minimal investment in walls.

4.  **Impenetrable Scorpion Defense:** In the Castle Age, Khmer scorpions get +1 range, bringing them to 8 range. This outranges almost every standard unit from that age (crossbows, cavalry archers, mangonels). Combined with the Ballistics technology, their scorpions create a defensive wall that is nearly impossible to break, forcing attackers to retreat while taking heavy damage.

**End:** The host concludes by acknowledging the Khmer's minor late-game weaknesses (like missing key infantry and archer technologies) but points out that their powerful elephants serve as the "finishing blow" after they've already dominated the entire game. The ultimate takeaway is that the Khmer's strength is unlocked by understanding and using their unique bonuses correctly, making them a powerhouse from the very start. The video ends with a final call to action to subscribe and support the creator on Patreon.

### 3. Key Insights

1.  **Early-Game Bonuses > Late-Game Power:** Small economic advantages in the early game have a much greater impact than large bonuses in the late game, as they dictate the entire tempo and flow of the match.
2.  **Tempo is Everything:** The Khmer's ability to produce units just a few seconds faster than their opponent is the difference between being the attacker and the defender, a massive advantage in pro play.
3.  **Defense Can Be an Offensive Weapon:** The Khmer's unique defensive capabilities (house hopping, long-range scorpions) are so effective that they frustrate opponents, drain their resources, and create opportunities for the Khmer player to gain an advantage without even attacking.
4.  **A Civ's True Strength Can Be Hidden:** The Khmer are a prime example of a civilization whose power isn't in their flashy unique unit but in subtle, synergistic bonuses that require knowledge to exploit fully.
