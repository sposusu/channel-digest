# WAN Show：越南管制YouTube廣告，迪士尼AI秀下限，最速Windows版本揭曉

## 重點摘要
本集節目深入探討了越南政府對YouTube等平台祭出的廣告新規定，限制了不可跳過廣告的長度，引發了關於平台可持續性的討論。同時，一項橫跨數個世代的Windows效能測試揭曉了意外的冠軍——Windows 8.1，並揭示了迪士尼一場令人尷尬的AI生成生物展示，引發了對AI與影視創作未來的反思。

## 故事大綱
- **開場**：主持人Linus和Luke開場，預告了本週的熱門話題：越南對YouTube廣告的嚴格管制、三星電視被爆監控用戶觀看內容、哪個Windows版本運行最快，以及迪士尼一場堪稱災難的AI技術展示。
- **中段**：
    - **越南 vs. YouTube**：節目首先深入討論越南的新法規，該法規限制了不可跳過廣告的長度，主持人探討了這對YouTube的商業模式和盈利能力的潛在衝擊。這也引出了一段關於YouTube市場壟斷地位的討論，並意外地讓他們去探索了至今依然存在的競爭對手Dailymotion。
    - **最速Windows揭曉**：接著，節目介紹了一項有趣的實驗，測試了從Windows XP到Windows 11等多個版本的效能。結果出人意料，在開機速度、磁碟使用率和記憶體效率等綜合測試中，被許多人遺忘的Windows 8.1在舊硬體上表現最佳，勝過了更新的Windows 10和11。
    - **迪士尼的AI鬧劇**：最後，主持人嚴厲批評了迪士尼一名高層在TED演講中展示的一段AI生成的「外星生物」影片。這些生物被嘲笑為「斑馬猩猩」和「蝸牛鳥」等低劣的混合體，完全缺乏創意。這段影片引發了關於AI在電影製作中真正角色、CGI與實體特效之爭，以及大公司盲目追捧AI潮流的廣泛討論。
- **結尾**：節目尾聲簡要提到了《柏德之門3》開發商Larian Studios對其在遊戲開發中使用生成式AI的立場澄清，最後進入了商品推銷環節。

## 關鍵見解
1. **政府監管挑戰科技巨頭**：越南的廣告新規定顯示，各國政府正開始挑戰科技平台的商業模式，用戶體驗正成為監管機構關注的重點，這可能迫使YouTube等公司改變其廣告策略。
2. **軟體並非越新越好**：Windows 8.1在測試中勝出，證明了更現代、功能更臃腫的作業系統在效能上不一定優於更輕量、更精簡的舊版本，尤其是在非頂級硬體上。
3. **AI無法取代真正創意**：迪士尼的失敗展示是一個警示，證明了當前AI工具在創意生成上的局限性。盲目地用AI取代人類藝術家的願景，只會產生缺乏靈魂和想像力的低品質內容。
4. **實體特效的藝術價值回歸**：主持人討論到，儘管CGI技術先進，但觀眾和創作者越來越欣賞實體特效（如真實的車輛追逐、模型爆破）所帶來的「重量感」和藝術性，因為它體現了真實的工藝和風險。
5. **YouTube的壟斷難以動搖**：在討論競爭對手時，主持人發現像Dailymotion這樣的平台雖然還存在，但無論在用戶數量還是功能上都遠遠無法與YouTube抗衡，凸顯了YouTube在影片分享領域的絕對主導地位。

## 精彩時刻
- 主持人抱著考古的心情探索Dailymotion網站，驚訝地發現它不僅還活著，甚至模仿YouTube推出了「Shorts」短影音功能，並找到了自己多年前被遺棄的頻道，上面僅有357個粉絲。
- 在觀看迪士尼AI影片前，Linus以為會是粉絲創作的劣質作品，沒想到官方發布的內容比他想像的還要尷尬和缺乏水準。
- 對AI生成生物的無情嘲諷，如「斑馬猩猩」、「蝸牛鳥」、「身上有金屬水滴的樹懶」，精準點評了其設計的荒謬性。
- 回憶Windows各個版本的歷史，眾人一致認為Windows 8是個「燃燒的垃圾桶」，但Windows 8.1卻是一個修正後極其出色且高效的「神級」作業系統。

# WAN Show: Vietnam vs. YouTube Ads, Disney's AI Fail & The Fastest Windows Revealed

## TL;DR
The hosts discuss Vietnam's new regulations limiting unskippable ads on platforms like YouTube, sparking a debate on platform sustainability. Meanwhile, a comprehensive benchmark test reveals the surprising fastest version of Windows is 8.1, and Disney gets ridiculed for a bizarre and uninspired AI-generated creature showcase.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning**: Linus and Luke kick off the show, outlining the main topics: Vietnam's crackdown on YouTube ads, the fastest version of Windows, and a cringeworthy AI presentation from Disney.
- **Middle**:
    - **Vietnam vs. YouTube**: They first tackle Vietnam's new rules limiting unskippable ads, debating the implications for YouTube's profitability and sustainability. This sparks a detour into YouTube's market dominance and a humorous investigation into Dailymotion, which is somehow still active.
    - **The Fastest Windows**: The conversation then shifts to a benchmark battle between Windows versions from XP to 11. Surprisingly, the much-maligned Windows 8.1 emerges as the fastest on the test hardware in a combination of boot time, disk usage, and memory efficiency.
    - **Disney's AI Folly**: Finally, they roast a Disney executive's TED talk that featured a montage of uninspired AI-generated "alien creatures" (e.g., "snail bird," "zebra gorilla"). This leads to a broader discussion on AI's role in filmmaking, practical effects vs. CGI, and corporate trend-chasing.
- **End**: The show wraps up with a look at Larian Studios' (Baldur's Gate 3) clarification on their use of generative AI in game development and concludes with a promotion for their merchandise.

## Key Insights
1.  **Government Regulation is Challenging Big Tech**: Vietnam's ad rules show that governments are starting to push back against the business models of tech platforms, prioritizing user experience and potentially forcing companies like YouTube to adapt their ad strategies.
2.  **Newer Isn't Always Better in Software**: Windows 8.1's victory in the benchmark proves that modern, bloated operating systems aren't always superior in performance to older, lighter alternatives, especially on less powerful hardware.
3.  **AI Can't Replace Genuine Creativity**: Disney's failed presentation is a cautionary tale about the current limitations of generative AI. Blindly replacing an artist's vision with AI tools can result in soulless, unimaginative, and low-quality content.
4.  **The Artistic Value of Practical Effects**: The hosts highlight a growing appreciation for the "weight" and artistry of practical effects (like real car chases and models). They offer a sense of realism and craftsmanship that CGI often struggles to replicate.
5.  **YouTube's Monopoly is Firm**: The discussion about competitors revealed that platforms like Dailymotion, despite still existing, are nowhere near challenging YouTube's dominance, highlighting the entrenched nature of its monopoly.

## Notable Moments
-   The hosts' archeological dig into the Dailymotion website, where they marveled at its continued existence, its clone of YouTube's features, and their own long-abandoned channel with 357 followers.
-   Linus recounting his expectation that the "Disney AI cringe" was going to be bad fanfiction, only to be shocked that the official presentation was far worse.
-   The incredulous descriptions of the AI creatures, such as the "snail bird" and the "zebra gorilla," mocking their lazy and uncreative design.
-   The nostalgic but critical look back at different Windows versions, with the consensus that Windows 8 was a "giant steaming dumpster fire" but the fixed 8.1 was a "goated" (great) and efficient OS.
