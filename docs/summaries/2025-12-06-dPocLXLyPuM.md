# 世紀帝國2新海戰重製深度剖析：理論 vs. 現實

## 重點摘要
世紀帝國2的海戰重製，意圖增加遊戲的策略深度與樂趣，但實際上並未達成目標。儘管帶來了一些好的改動（如簡潔的船塢介面），但核心玩法依然單一且極度「滾雪球」，感覺只是將舊問題換了一層新皮，並未真正解決根本弊病。

## 故事大綱
- **開場**：影片作者（一名職業選手）介紹世紀帝國2正在一個大型賽事中測試的全新海戰重製內容。在深入遊玩（打進16強）後，他將分享他對這次改動的詳細看法。
- **中段**：他首先快速回顧了重製的幾大變動：新增了近戰船艦「浩克」（Hulk）、形成「槳帆船 > 浩克 > 火長船」的新三角相剋關係、削弱了爆破船、並將所有海軍科技移至學院。接著，他提出核心論點：這次重製「沒有抓住重點」。他逐一分析理論與實踐的差距，指出新系統非但沒有增加策略性，反而因為移除了爆破船這項反制機制，讓海戰變得極度滾雪球，一旦落後就難以翻身。同時，所有科技集中在學院，反而扼殺了開局的策略多樣性。
- **結尾**：作者總結，重製的想法雖好，但執行效果不佳。海戰的核心問題——單一兵種互毆、勝負取決於數量——依然存在。他建議開發團隊移除「浩克」這項畫蛇添足的設計，但保留部分平衡性調整與介面優化，並對海戰玩法進行更深入的思考。

## 關鍵見解
- **新單位未增策略深度**：新增的「浩克」船並沒有創造出動態的策略組合，只是取代了舊 meta（玩法結構）中的火長船，導致玩法依舊單一：開放海域還是槳帆船海，而封閉水域則變成浩克海。
- **滾雪球效應加劇**：透過獨立升級來削弱爆破船，雖是合理改動，卻移除了遊戲中唯一能以小博大、劣勢翻盤的關鍵單位。這使得海戰變得極度滾雪球化，前期取得微小優勢的一方幾乎必勝。
- **科技集中扼殺多樣性**：將所有海軍科技移至學院，本意是增加決策點，實際上卻適得其反。由於新科技過於強大，導致所有玩家在海圖上被迫選擇「直衝學院」的單一開局，反而喪失了原有的策略多樣性。
- **核心問題依舊存在**：海戰的根本問題在於，戰鬥往往簡化為單一兵種的數量比拚，而非兵種搭配或操作技巧。這次重製只是「換了新的兵種皮膚」，卻沒有改變這個沉悶的核心。
- **唯一公認的優點**：唯一獲得普遍好評的改動是將船塢介面簡化為單頁，這項使用者體驗的優化非常成功。

## 精彩時刻
- 經典吐槽：「這次重製只是讓事情『看起來不同』，但『感覺起來完全一樣』。」
- 形容當前的海戰：「玩家們現在就是把船巡邏過去，然後祈禱能打贏。」
- 對於爆破船的評價：「爆破船的存在，為遊戲增添了一種『策略性的恐懼』，你必須時時尊重它的威脅。」
- 最直接的建議：「把浩克移除掉吧。」

# Age of Empires 2 Water Rework: Good Ideas, Flawed Execution

## TL;DR
The Age of Empires 2 water rework, intended to add strategic depth and fun, ultimately fails to achieve its goals. While some quality-of-life changes like the new dock UI are excellent, the core gameplay remains one-dimensional and extremely "snowbally," feeling more like a reskin of old problems than a true solution.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning:** The speaker, a professional player, introduces the new water rework being tested in a major tournament. After playing extensively and reaching the top 16, he's ready to share his in-depth opinion.
- **Middle:** He first recaps the major changes: the new melee "Hulk" unit, a new rock-paper-scissors dynamic (Galley > Hulk > Fire Ship), nerfed Demolition Ships, and moving all ship upgrades to the University. He then delivers his core thesis: the rework "missed the mark." He breaks down, point by point, how the intended theory doesn't match the reality of gameplay. Instead of adding strategy, the changes have made water even more snowbally (due to the lack of a comeback mechanic like Demos) and have stifled strategic diversity by forcing a University-first opening on all water maps.
- **End:** He concludes that the ideas were good on paper but flawed in execution. The fundamental problems of water gameplay—massing one unit type and snowballing from an early lead—remain. He suggests removing the new Hulk unit, keeping the smaller balance tweaks and UI improvements, and calling for a more thoughtful approach to fixing water.

## Key Insights
1.  **New Unit, Same Strategy:** The new Hulk unit didn't create dynamic strategies; it just replaced the old meta. Gameplay remains one-dimensional: Galleys still dominate open water, while Hulks now dominate ponds and closed maps.
2.  **Extreme Snowball Effect:** Nerfing Demolition Ships by giving them a separate upgrade removed the primary comeback mechanic that allowed for "value trades." This makes water combat extremely snowbally, where a small early lead often guarantees victory.
3.  **Forced Strategy, Less Diversity:** Moving all ship upgrades to the University backfired. Instead of creating more choices, the power of these techs makes building a University a mandatory opening, killing the strategic diversity that previously existed.
4.  **The Core Problem Persists:** The fundamental issue with water combat—that it's about massing a superior number of a single unit rather than tactical composition—has not been fixed. The rework is a "new skin on an old problem."
5.  **The Unanimous Positive:** The single-page Dock UI is a universally praised quality-of-life improvement, making ship production and upgrades much clearer and more accessible.

## Notable Moments
- The key takeaway quote: "The rework just makes things look different but they feel the same."
- On how current water fights feel: Players are just "patrolling in and they're just praying."
- On the role of old Demolition Ships: "Demos added that kind of strategic fear... that respect that you always have to consider."
- His most direct suggestion for a fix: "Remove the Hulk."
