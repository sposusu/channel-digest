# 將遊戲化為現實：模組改造值得嗎？

## 重點摘要
這部影片探討了將《電馭叛客2077》、《上古卷軸V：無界天際》和《末日之戰》等遊戲透過大量模組（Mods）改造以達到「照片級真實感」的過程。結論是，雖然結果在特定場景下令人驚豔，但過程耗時、複雜且對硬體要求極高，並且效果往往是精心挑選下的成果，不一定在實際遊戲中能持續。

## 故事大綱
- **開場：** 影片以一個問題開場：為了追求極致畫質而改造遊戲是否值得？團隊決定親身實驗，嘗試在幾款熱門遊戲上重現網路上瘋傳的照片級真實畫面。
- **中段：**
    - **《電馭叛客2077》**：安裝了 72 個模組，主要集中在路徑追蹤、高解析度材質和光影效果。在特定場景下（如下雨的夜晚），畫面極其逼真，但也揭示了這需要消耗大量 VRAM（超過 16GB），且效果在一般移動和非特定場景下會打折扣。
    - **《俠盜獵車手V》**：改造嘗試完全失敗，因為遊戲最近的一次更新破壞了關鍵的模組工具（Script Hook V），凸顯了改造線上服務型遊戲的風險。
    - **《上古卷軸V：無界天際》**：使用模組管理器和 ENB 工具，團隊成功將這款老遊戲的畫面提升到現代水準，特別是在植被、水體和光影方面。但過程同樣充滿挑戰，尤其是在處理模組的載入順序和衝突上。
    - **《末日之戰 (原版)》**：作為額外測試，團隊使用了一個整合包，輕鬆地大幅提升了遊戲畫面，證明了老遊戲透過模組改造的巨大潛力。
- **結尾：** 主持人 Linus 親身體驗後，對《無界天際》的改造效果印象深刻，但也指出這些「真實」畫面很多時候是「精挑細選」的結果。團隊的結論是，模組改造適合在第二輪或多年後重玩時嘗試，而付費模組包通常只是免費模組的集合，鼓勵玩家自己研究。

## 關鍵見解
1.  **高昂的時間成本：** 實現照片級畫質需要花費大量時間研究、安裝、測試和解決模組衝突，光是《無界天際》就花了一天半的時間。
2.  **效果是「精挑細選」的：** 網路瘋傳的驚豔畫面通常是在特定地點、天氣和光線下才有的效果，實際遊玩時很難全程保持。
3.  **硬體是硬傷：** 開啟路徑追蹤和高解析度材質會急遽增加 VRAM 的使用量，即使是頂級顯卡在非 4K 解析度下也可能被佔滿。
4.  **老遊戲比新遊戲更適合改造：** 像《無界天-際》這樣不再頻繁更新的單機遊戲，其模組社群更成熟、穩定，不像《俠盜獵車手V》那樣容易因官方更新而失效。
5.  **付費模組包可能不值：** 許多付費模組包只是將 Nexus Mods 上的免費模組打包，再加上一個自訂的畫面濾鏡（ReShade 預設），真正的價值在於省去研究時間，但內容本身是免費的。

## 精彩時刻
- 模組衝突的載入順序圖看起來像一張複雜的蜘蛛網，讓人頭暈。
- 《俠盜獵車手V》的改造計畫因一個小小的官方更新而徹底泡湯。
- 展示《無界天際》原版與改造後的對比時，原版的畫面（特別是水）被毫不留情地評價為「看起來糟透了」。
- 主持人 Linus 分享他曾經因為過度改造《遺忘之都》而遺失數十小時遊戲進度的慘痛經歷。
- 團隊將 Linus 現實生活中的保時捷 Taycan 做成模組放進了《電馭叛客2077》中，給了他一個大驚喜。

# Turning Games into Reality: Is Photorealistic Modding Worth It?

## TL;DR
This video explores the process of modding games like Cyberpunk 2077, Skyrim, and Crysis to achieve "photorealism." The conclusion is that while the results can be stunning in specific, cherry-picked scenarios, the process is time-consuming, complex, and extremely demanding on hardware, with an outcome that isn't always consistent during actual gameplay.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning:** The video starts by questioning if the effort to mod games for ultimate visual fidelity is worthwhile. The team decides to find out by trying to replicate the viral photorealistic gameplay seen online on several popular titles.
- **Middle:**
    - **Cyberpunk 2077:** They installed 72 mods focusing on path tracing, high-resolution textures, and lighting effects. In specific scenes (like a rainy night), the visuals were incredibly realistic but revealed a massive VRAM cost (over 16GB) and that the effect diminishes during normal traversal and in non-ideal scenes.
    - **GTA V:** The modding attempt failed completely because a recent game update broke a key modding tool (Script Hook V), highlighting the risks of modding live-service games.
    - **Skyrim:** Using a mod manager and a tool called ENB, the team successfully transformed the old game's graphics to a modern level, especially the foliage, water, and lighting. The process was still challenging, particularly with managing mod load orders and conflicts.
    - **Crysis (Original):** As a bonus, the team used an all-in-one enhancement pack that easily and significantly improved the game's visuals, demonstrating the huge potential for modding older titles.
- **End:** The host, Linus, reacts to the results. He's impressed by Skyrim's transformation but also points out that the "realism" is often highly cherry-picked. The team concludes that modding is great for a second playthrough or revisiting a game years later, and that paid mod packs are often just collections of free mods, encouraging viewers to do their own research.

## Key Insights
1.  **High Time Investment:** Achieving photorealism requires a significant amount of time for research, installation, testing, and troubleshooting mod conflicts. Skyrim alone took about a day and a half.
2.  **Results are "Cherry-Picked":** The stunning visuals that go viral are often captured in specific locations, weather, and lighting conditions. It's difficult to maintain that level of quality throughout regular gameplay.
3.  **Hardware is a Major Hurdle:** Enabling path tracing and high-resolution textures dramatically increases VRAM usage, potentially maxing out even high-end graphics cards at sub-4K resolutions.
4.  **Older Games are Better Candidates:** Single-player games that are no longer frequently updated, like Skyrim, have more mature and stable modding communities, unlike GTA V which can be easily broken by official updates.
5.  **Paid Mod Packs May Not Be Worth It:** Many paid packs simply bundle freely available mods from sites like Nexus Mods with a custom ReShade preset. You're paying for the convenience of a curated list, not for exclusive content.

## Notable Moments
- The mod conflict load order map was shown, looking like a complex and confusing spiderweb.
- The entire GTA V modding project was derailed by a single, minor game update.
- When comparing vanilla Skyrim to the modded version, the original graphics (especially the water) were bluntly described as looking "awful."
- The host, Linus, shared a story about losing tens of hours of progress in his heavily modded Oblivion save file.
- The team surprised Linus by modding his real-life Porsche Taycan into Cyberpunk 2077.
