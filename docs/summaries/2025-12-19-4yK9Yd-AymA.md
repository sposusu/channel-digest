# The WAN Show：Meta 廣告詐騙、微軟 Copilot 困境與遊戲業 AI 爭議

## 重點摘要
本集節目揭露了 Meta 如何為賺取數十億美元，而容忍其平台上的詐騙廣告。同時，微軟坦承其 AI 產品 Copilot 市場反應冷淡，難以吸引買家。節目後半段深入探討了《柏德之門3》開發商 Larian Studios 使用 AI 引發的爭議，引發關於 AI 在創意領域角色的激烈辯論。

## 故事大綱
- 開場：主持人以輕鬆的玩笑開場，隨即切入本週的重磅科技新聞，首先揭露 Meta 明知廣告存在詐騙，卻為了高達數十億美元的收入而選擇默許。
- 中段：話題轉向微軟，報導指出由於 Copilot 在實際辦公室任務中失敗率高達 70%，導致其成長目標被迫下修。這也引發了對現有 AI 助理（如 Siri）功能笨拙、不夠人性化的抱怨。隨後，特別來賓 Riley 加入，共同探討遊戲開發商 Larian Studios 因使用 AI 輔助概念設計而引發的社群強烈反彈。
- 結尾：對話聚焦於 Larian 的 AI 爭議，辯論 AI 究竟是提升效率的輔助工具，還是會取代人類藝術家、扼殺創意的拐杖。討論延伸至大眾對 AI 在創意領域應用的普遍恐懼，以及為何 Larian 這樣備受讚譽的公司會成為眾矢之的。

## 關鍵見解
1.  **巨型企業的免責特權**：像 Meta 這樣的大公司似乎可以凌駕於規則之上，為了利潤可以忽視平台上的詐騙行為和反垃圾郵件法規，展現了「大到不能管」的現實。
2.  **AI 產品的現實困境**：儘管市場大肆宣傳，但微軟 Copilot 等 AI 產品在實際應用中步履蹣跚，因為它們在真實世界任務中表現不佳，且未能提供比現有工具（如手機）更優越的體驗。
3.  **創意領域的 AI 倫理戰**：遊戲開發等創意產業成為 AI 倫理的戰場。儘管開發者聲稱 AI 僅用於輔助單調任務，但其在概念發想階段的應用，引發了取代藝術家、侵蝕人類創造力的深層恐懼。
4.  **責任感的「鐘形曲線」**：企業的規模決定了其面臨的監督壓力。小公司因無人關注而能僥倖，中型公司受到最嚴格的審查，而巨型企業則因其龐大規模，再次變得難以被有效約束。
5.  **AI 助理名不副實**：現今主流的 AI 助理（如 Siri）在理解自然語言和處理多步驟指令方面仍然非常笨拙，凸顯了 AI 行銷口號與用戶實際體驗之間的巨大落差。

## 精彩時刻
- Linus 對 Facebook 屢次發送無法取消訂閱的行銷郵件感到憤怒，並指出這公然違反了反垃圾郵件法。
- 主持人嘲諷微軟執行長 Satya Nadella 想把 Copilot「硬塞進你的喉嚨」。
- 特別來賓 Riley 的混亂登場，以及他堅持不使用椅子的搞笑橋段。
- 將對 AI 的恐懼比作歷史上的「計算機論證」——擔心新工具會讓人們喪失基本技能。
- 一位開發者為 AI 辯護時引用：「這種對 AI 的歇斯底里，就像 19 世紀人們砸毀蒸汽機一樣。」

# The WAN Show: Meta's Ad Fraud, Microsoft's Co-pilot Struggles, and the AI in Gaming Debate

## TL;DR
This episode covers how Meta knowingly profits from billions of dollars in fraudulent ads while ignoring regulations. Meanwhile, Microsoft admits its AI product, Co-pilot, is failing to gain traction with buyers. The show then dives deep into the controversy surrounding Larian Studios (Baldur's Gate 3) using AI, sparking a heated debate on technology's role in creativity.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning:** The show opens with lighthearted banter before diving into the week's major tech news, first revealing that Meta is knowingly earning billions from fraudulent ads, particularly from Chinese resellers, and choosing to tolerate it.
- **Middle:** The conversation pivots to a report that Microsoft is lowering its growth targets for Co-pilot due to low user interest and a reported 70% failure rate in real-world office tasks. This sparks a broader critique of the clunky, unnatural state of current AI assistants like Siri. Guest Riley then joins to discuss the community backlash against game developer Larian Studios for using AI to help with early concept ideation.
- **End:** The conversation focuses on the Larian situation, debating whether AI is a helpful tool that automates grunt work or a crutch that replaces human artists and stifles creativity. The discussion explores the public's intense, negative reaction to any mention of AI in creative fields and why a beloved studio like Larian became a target.

## Key Insights
1.  **Big Tech Impunity:** Massive companies like Meta can operate above the rules, prioritizing profit from fraudulent ads over user safety and legal compliance (like anti-spam laws), demonstrating a "too big to fail" reality.
2.  **The Reality of AI Products:** Despite immense hype, AI products like Microsoft's Co-pilot are struggling to meet market expectations because they perform poorly on real-world tasks and offer little significant advantage over existing, more reliable tools (like a smartphone).
3.  **The Ethical Battleground of Creative AI:** Creative industries like game development are a primary battleground for AI ethics. While developers claim AI is used for menial tasks, its use in conceptual stages (e.g., Larian) sparks deep fears of replacing artists and eroding human creativity.
4.  **The "Bell Curve of Accountability":** A company's size dictates the scrutiny it faces. Small companies fly under the radar, mid-sized companies are heavily policed, and the largest corporations become so massive they are once again difficult to effectively regulate or boycott.
5.  **AI Assistants Are Not There Yet:** Today's mainstream AI assistants (Siri, etc.) remain clunky and fail to understand natural, multi-part commands, highlighting the significant disconnect between AI marketing hype and the actual user experience.

## Notable Moments
- Linus's rant about Facebook repeatedly sending him marketing emails with no unsubscribe link, in clear violation of anti-spam laws.
- The hosts joking about Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella wanting to "cram [Co-pilot] down your throat."
- Guest Riley's chaotic entrance and the running gag about him refusing to use a stool.
- The comparison of fears about AI to the historical "calculator argument"—the worry that new tools will cause people to lose fundamental skills.
- A developer's quote defending AI use: "This AI hysteria is the same as when people were smashing steam engines in the 19th century."
