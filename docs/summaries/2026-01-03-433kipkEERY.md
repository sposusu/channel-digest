# WAN 秀：重大所有權變革、舊硬體的價值與科技屋的恐怖發現

## 重點摘要
本集節目中，Linus 宣布了一個重大消息：WAN 秀現在由他和 Luke 以 50/50 的形式共同擁有，以確保其長遠的未來。此外，主持人強烈倡導「廢品站戰爭」(Scrapyard Wars) 精神，認為在當前硬體價格飛漲的時期，購買和使用二手舊硬體是一種聰明且值得尊敬的選擇。

## 故事大綱
- 開場：節目從討論 90 年代的 CGI 動畫影集《Reboot》的數位修復計畫開始，並提及 LTT 多年來一直在支持這個媒體保存專案。主持人也簡短抱怨了 GPU 價格上漲和 Grok AI 生成不當圖片等科技新聞。
- 中段：首先，主持人深入探討了「廢品站戰爭」的理念，以應對昂貴的新 GPU。他們指出，像 Ryzen 5800X 和 GTX 1080 這樣的老舊硬體在今天仍然堪用，並鼓勵觀眾透過組裝「過渡期」電腦來省錢。接著，節目迎來最重大的宣布：Linus 和 Luke 成立了一家名為「Adequate Media Inc.」的新公司，兩人各持股 50%，共同擁有 WAN 秀，以確保節目能永久地持續下去。最後，他们討論了美國 FCC 出於國安考量而禁止外國製造無人機（特別是 DJI）的舉措及其潛在的負面影響。
- 結尾：Linus 分享了「科技屋」(Tech House) 的最新進度。他們已經取得了房屋所有權，這個房子將作為未來各種科技產品部署的「測試平台」。他描述了前屋主留下的各種奇怪甚至危險的 DIY 改造，例如在廁所旁的電話線插孔和有安全隱患的 240 伏特延長線，以及最近才發現的房屋地基和排水問題。

## 關鍵見解
- **WAN 秀的未來得到保障**：節目現在由 Linus 和 Luke 平等共同擁有，這是一個象徵性及實質性的举措，旨在確保无论他们未来职业如何变化，WAN 秀都能長久存在。
- **「廢品站戰爭」的智慧**：在硬體昂貴的時代，购买和利用二手零件来组建夠用的電腦，不仅是可行的，更是一种值得推崇的、充滿樂趣的DIY精神。
- **媒體保存的重要性**：透過《Reboot》修復計畫的例子，凸顯了保存早期數位媒體的挑戰與價值，這些內容是重要的文化和技術歷史資產。
- **科技禁令的雙面刃**：美國對 DJI 等無人機的禁令，雖然有其國安考量，但也可能扼殺市場競爭，並對依赖这些技术的行业造成衝擊，其背後更可能有政治动机。
- **DIY 的黑暗面**：科技屋的案例顯示，外表看似正常的房屋底下，可能充滿了前人留下的大量不安全、不合規範的 DIY 改造，這也成為新專案的挑戰與樂趣所在。

## 精彩時刻
- Linus 正式宣布：「WAN 秀是我和 Luke 之間 50/50 的合作夥伴關係。」
- 關於「廢品站戰爭」精神：「喚醒你內心的廢品站戰士，是度過這樣時期的好方法。」
- 科技屋的怪異發現：前屋主在馬桶旁邊安裝了一個 RJ11 電話線插孔。
- 關於前屋主危险的 DIY：「他有一个德语的标志，警告不要将烘干机和车库里的那个（240V 插座）同时运行。」

# WAN Show: Major Ownership Change, The Value of Old Hardware & Tech House Horrors

## TL;DR
In this episode, Linus makes a major announcement: The WAN Show is now co-owned 50/50 by him and Luke to secure its long-term future. Additionally, the hosts strongly advocate for the "Scrapyard Wars" ethos, arguing that buying and using older, second-hand hardware is a smart and honorable choice amid soaring market prices.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning:** The show kicks off with a discussion about the digital remastering project for the '90s CGI series "Reboot," mentioning LTT's long-term support for this media preservation effort. The hosts also briefly touch on tech news like rising GPU prices and the Grok AI controversy.
- **Middle:** The hosts transition to the main topics. First, they make a detailed case for the "Scrapyard Wars" philosophy to counter expensive new GPUs, noting that older hardware like the Ryzen 5800X and GTX 1080 is still perfectly viable today and encouraging viewers to build "stop-gap" systems. This leads to the biggest announcement: Linus and Luke have formed a new company, "Adequate Media Inc.," to co-own the WAN Show in a 50/50 partnership, safeguarding its future indefinitely. Finally, they discuss the US FCC's ban on foreign-made drones (notably DJI) over national security concerns and its potential negative consequences.
- **End:** Linus gives an update on the "Tech House," a property they have acquired to serve as a "test bench" for future tech deployments. He describes the bizarre and sometimes unsafe DIY tech left by the previous owner, such as an RJ11 phone jack next to the toilet, a questionable 240-volt extension cord setup, and recently discovered foundation and drainage issues.

## Key Insights
- **The WAN Show's Future is Secured:** The show is now an equal partnership between Linus and Luke, a symbolic and practical move to ensure its longevity regardless of their future career paths.
- **The Wisdom of "Scrapyard Wars":** In an era of expensive hardware, building a capable PC with used parts is not only feasible but also a respectable and enjoyable expression of the DIY spirit.
- **The Importance of Media Preservation:** The "Reboot" remastering project highlights the challenges and value of preserving early digital media, which stands as an important part of cultural and technological history.
- **The Double-Edged Sword of Tech Bans:** The US ban on drones like DJI, while citing national security, could stifle market competition and negatively impact industries that rely on the technology, with potential political motives behind it.
- **The Dark Side of DIY:** The Tech House project reveals that beneath a normal-looking home can lie a legacy of unsafe and non-compliant DIY modifications, which becomes both a challenge and a source of content.

## Notable Moments
- The big reveal: "The WAN show is a 50/50 partnership between me and Luke."
- The "Scrapyard Wars" ethos: "Channeling your inner scrapyard warrior is a great way to ride out times like this."
- The bizarre tech house find: The previous owner installed an RJ11 (phone) jack next to the toilet.
- On the previous owner's dangerous DIY: "He had a sign above the dryer in German not to run it at the same time as the one in the garage."
