# 2025年市場回顧與2026年展望：AI、川普與地緣政治

## 重點摘要
2025年市場由AI主導，呈現上半年熊市、下半年牛市的V型反轉，但漲幅高度集中於少數科技股。展望2026年，市場焦點將從經濟韌性轉向川普政府帶來的極高政治不確定性，預期將是一個波動劇烈、感受上像熊市，但實際上可能持續走高的牛市。

## 故事大綱
- **開場**：回顧2025年，市場波動極大，上半年為熊市，下半年因AI熱潮轉為牛市。黃金是年度表現最佳資產（+64.6%），比特幣則是唯一錄得負報酬的主流資產（-5.9%），與年初的高度期望形成強烈對比。
- **中段**：分析指出，美股漲勢過度集中，市場廣度不足，多數股票及對沖基金均難以跑贏大盤，而海外市場（如韓國、中國）表現反而更亮眼。展望2026年，分析師Tom Lee認為結構性缺工將迫使企業長期投資科技，支撐AI多頭。然而，市場將面臨川普政府帶來的巨大政治風險，包括其直接干預藥價、強硬外交施壓（委內瑞拉、敘利亞），以及艾普斯坦文件等政治事件的衝擊。
- **結尾**：總結認為，2026年的投資關鍵在於能否承受川普政策引發的政治波動。市場可能「看起來像熊市，走起來是牛市」。建議投資者應專注於結構性趨勢（如科技自動化），而非追逐短期指數高低，並在市場悲觀時分批佈局。

## 關鍵見解
1.  **結構性多頭 vs. 週期性風險**：長期來看，全球勞動力短缺將持續推動企業對科技與自動化的投資，這為AI產業提供了結構性的長期支撐，而非短期的景氣循環。
2.  **政治凌駕經濟**：2026年的市場主要風險來自政治，特別是川普的關稅、移民、藥價及外交政策，其不可預測性將顯著放大市場波動。
3.  **高度集中的回報**：市場廣度持續收窄，報酬高度集中於少數科技巨頭。這使得單純持有大盤ETF越來越難跑贏，即便是頂級對沖基金也面臨嚴峻挑戰。
4.  **全球資金輪動**：2025年，許多海外市場（如韓國、希臘、中國）的表現優於美國。隨著美中關係緩和，資金明顯從前幾年大熱的印度股市流向中國股市。
5.  **川普的直接干預**：川普政府繞過傳統經濟工具，直接與企業協商降價（如藥廠）、調查物價、放鬆大麻管制，以應對通膨壓力與爭取民心。

# 2025 Market Review & 2026 Outlook: AI, Trump, and Geopolitics

## TL;DR
The 2025 market executed a V-shaped recovery driven by AI, moving from a bear to a bull market, but with gains highly concentrated in a few tech stocks. Looking ahead to 2026, the focus shifts from economic resilience to extreme political uncertainty driven by the Trump administration, promising a volatile "bearish-feeling bull market" that may continue to trend upward.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning**: The video reviews the highly volatile 2025 market, which was a bear market in the first half and a bull market in the second, thanks to the AI boom. Gold was the year's top-performing asset (+64.6%), while Bitcoin was the only major asset to post a negative return (-5.9%), a stark contrast to the high expectations at the start of the year.
- **Middle**: The analysis points out that the US stock market rally is overly concentrated with poor market breadth, making it difficult for most stocks and even hedge funds to beat the market. Meanwhile, international markets (like South Korea and China) performed better. For 2026, analyst Tom Lee argues that a structural labor shortage will force long-term investment in technology, supporting the AI bull case. However, the market will face immense political risk from the Trump administration, including direct intervention in drug prices, aggressive foreign policy (Venezuela, Syria), and the fallout from political events like the Epstein files.
- **End**: The segment concludes that the key to investing in 2026 will be the ability to withstand political volatility driven by Trump. The market might "look like a bear market but act like a bull market." Investors are advised to focus on structural trends (like tech automation) rather than short-term index levels and to build positions during periods of market pessimism.

## Key Insights
1.  **Structural Bull vs. Cyclical Risk**: In the long term, a global labor shortage will drive sustained corporate investment in technology and automation, providing structural, long-term support for the AI industry that transcends short-term economic cycles.
2.  **Politics Over Economics**: The primary market risk for 2026 is political, specifically the unpredictability of Trump's policies on tariffs, immigration, drug prices, and foreign affairs, which will significantly amplify market volatility.
3.  **Highly Concentrated Returns**: Market breadth continues to narrow, with returns highly concentrated in a few tech giants. This makes it increasingly difficult for broad-market ETFs to outperform and poses a severe challenge even for top hedge funds.
4.  **Global Capital Rotation**: In 2025, many international markets (e.g., South Korea, Greece, China) outperformed the US. As US-China relations thawed, capital notably rotated from the previously hot Indian market to the Chinese market.
5.  **Trump's Direct Intervention**: The Trump administration is bypassing traditional economic levers, opting for direct action such as negotiating price cuts with companies (like pharma), investigating price hikes, and easing cannabis regulations to manage inflation and public opinion.
