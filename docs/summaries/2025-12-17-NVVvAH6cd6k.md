# Valve 新款 VR 頭戴「Steam Frame」深度評測：市場顛覆者還是高價玩物？

## 重點摘要
Valve 推出新款 VR 頭戴「Steam Frame」，憑藉輕巧設計、眼動追蹤及創新的「注視點串流」技術，在無線 PC VR 體驗上超越對手。然而，其 LCD 螢幕與高昂的預期售價，使其在頂級畫質與性價比方面面臨挑戰。

## 故事大綱
- **開場：** 講者受邀至 Valve 總部體驗全新的 Steam Frame，但因時間倉促，決定回到工作室，將其與市面上所有主流 VR 頭戴（如 Valve Index、Meta Quest 3、Apple Vision Pro、Big Screen Beyond 2、PSVR 2）進行詳細的橫向評測。
- **中段：** 影片逐一比較各家頭戴的優劣。Steam Frame 在重量、配重、無線串流技術（透過注視點串流）上表現出色，超越了 Quest 3 和笨重的 Vision Pro。然而，在螢幕對比度上，它不如採用 Micro-OLED 的 Vision Pro 和 Big Screen Beyond 2；在視野和音效方面，可能與自家的舊款 Index 持平或稍遜；在價格上，遠不如 PSVR 2 親民。
- **結尾：** 講者預測 Steam Frame 售價可能接近 1000 美元。儘管價格不菲且螢幕非 OLED，但他認為憑藉其開放生態、強大的軟體相容性（可玩 Windows 遊戲）與創新的串流技術，該產品依然極具吸引力，並表示將在發售日當天購買。

## 關鍵見解
1.  **注視點串流 (Foveated Streaming) 是最大亮點：** 利用眼動追蹤，只在使用者注視的區域渲染最高畫質，大幅提升無線 PC VR 的效率與品質，是超越 Meta Quest 的關鍵技術。
2.  **重量與人體工學是核心優勢：** Frame 將電池後置，實現了優異的重量分佈和僅 440 克的總重，舒適度遠勝於 Quest 3 和極其沉重的 Apple Vision Pro。
3.  **畫質並非頂級：** 仍採用 LCD 螢幕，意味著其對比度和色彩表現將輸給使用 (Micro-)OLED 的 Apple Vision Pro、Big Screen Beyond 2 和 PSVR 2。
4.  **生態系統的開放性是隱形價值：** 與 Meta 和 Apple 的封閉生態不同，Valve 允許使用者遊玩各種平台的遊戲（ARM、Linux、Windows），並提供擴充接口，賦予其更高的自由度和潛力。
5.  **價格將是關鍵因素：** 雖然產品力強大，但接近 1000 美元的預期售價將使其成為高端玩家的選擇，而非像 Quest 系列那樣能普及市場的產品。

## 精彩時刻
- 講者抱怨在 Valve 總部只有不到兩小時體驗時間，還要寫稿拍片，結果錯過了飛機。
- 戲稱 Apple Vision Pro 是「空間運算電腦」，但「到現在都沒人知道那到底是啥鬼」。
- 形容 Big Screen Beyond 2 是用「鑲鑽金釘」打造的極致輕量化產品。
- 熱血沸騰地表示，即使 Steam Frame 賣 1000 美元，他也會在發售日當天立刻下單。

# Valve's New "Steam Frame" VR Headset Review: A Market Disruptor or a Premium Toy?

## TL;DR
Valve's new Steam Frame headset excels in wireless PC VR with a lightweight design, eye-tracking, and innovative "foveated streaming." However, its use of LCD panels and a high expected price point present challenges against competitors offering superior image quality or better value.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning:** The speaker visits Valve HQ for a brief, rushed hands-on with the new Steam Frame. Frustrated by the lack of time, he returns to his studio to conduct a comprehensive comparison against all major VR headsets on the market (Valve Index, Meta Quest 3, Apple Vision Pro, Big Screen Beyond 2, PSVR 2).
- **Middle:** The video breaks down the pros and cons of each headset versus the Frame. The Steam Frame shines with its low weight, balanced design (battery in the back), and superior wireless streaming technology (foveated streaming), outclassing the Quest 3 and the cumbersome Vision Pro. However, it falls short in screen contrast compared to the Micro-OLED displays of the Vision Pro and Big Screen Beyond 2, may only match its predecessor (the Index) in FoV and audio, and is far from the budget-friendly PSVR 2.
- **End:** The speaker predicts the Steam Frame will cost close to $1,000. Despite the high price and non-OLED screen, he concludes that its open ecosystem, powerful software compatibility (playing Windows games), and innovative streaming tech make it a highly compelling product that he will buy on day one.

## Key Insights
1.  **Foveated Streaming is the Killer Feature:** Using eye-tracking to render only the user's focal point at max quality dramatically boosts the efficiency and fidelity of wireless PC VR, giving it a key advantage over Meta Quest.
2.  **Ergonomics and Weight are a Core Advantage:** By moving the battery to the rear, the Frame achieves excellent weight distribution at only 440g total, making it far more comfortable than the front-heavy Quest 3 and the incredibly heavy Apple Vision Pro.
3.  **Image Quality Isn't Top-Tier:** The continued use of LCD panels means its contrast and color will be inferior to headsets using (Micro-)OLED, such as the Apple Vision Pro, Big Screen Beyond 2, and PSVR 2.
4.  **Open Ecosystem is a Hidden Value:** Unlike the walled gardens of Meta and Apple, Valve allows users to play games from various platforms (ARM, Linux, Windows) and provides an expansion port, offering greater freedom and potential.
5.  **Price Will Be a Deciding Factor:** While powerful, the expected ~$1,000 price tag positions it as a choice for high-end enthusiasts rather than a mass-market product like the Quest series.

- Joking that the Apple Vision Pro is a "spatial computer," but "nobody even now knows what the [hell] that is."
- Describing the Big Screen Beyond 2 as nailing its lightweight goal with "diamond encrusted golden nails."
- His emphatic statement that even if the Steam Frame costs $1,000, he's "going to be buying one on launch day."
