# 為什麼你的顯示卡這麼貴？

## 重點摘要
電腦（特別是顯示卡）價格不斷飆升，其根本原因在於半導體供應鏈中環環相扣的壟斷現象。從 NVIDIA 的市場主導，到台積電的先進製程，再到 ASML 的獨家設備，每一層都缺乏有效競爭，導致消費者最終得承擔所有成本。

## 故事大綱
- **開場：** 影片以一個充滿戲劇張力的短劇開場，直接點名 NVIDIA，質疑其高昂的 GPU 定價。接著揭示 NVIDIA 自身沒有晶圓廠（Fab），其晶片完全依賴台灣的台積電（TSMC）製造。
- **中段：** 影片深入挖掘，指出台積電的龍頭地位，源於其掌握了最先進的晶片製造技術，特別是「極紫外光微影技術」（EUV）。然而，製造晶片的機器（光刻機）本身，全球只有一家荷蘭公司 ASML 能夠提供。一台 EUV 光刻機要價高達 2 億美元，形成了「壟斷中的壟斷」。
- **結尾：** 影片總結，從晶片設計（NVIDIA）、晶片製造（台積電）到設備供應（ASML），供應鏈的每一個關鍵節點都由一家公司獨霸。加上 AI 熱潮對高階晶片的龐大需求，更加劇了價格上漲。儘管未來可能有新的競爭者出現，但短期內高昂的價格恐將持續。

## 關鍵見解
1.  **層層堆疊的壟斷：** 高昂的硬體價格並非單一公司的問題，而是一個由 NVIDIA（設計端近乎壟斷）、台積電（製造端近乎壟斷）和 ASML（設備端完全壟斷）組成的產業結構性問題。
2.  **ASML 的絕對關鍵地位：** ASML 是全球唯一能製造 EUV 光刻機的公司，這些機器是生產尖端晶片的必要工具。一台新機器的價格甚至高達 4 億美元，連台積電都感到壓力。
3.  **競爭的缺乏是主因：** 在供應鏈的每一個環節，都缺少能制衡價格的競爭者。AMD 和 Intel 在 GPU 市場上遠遠落後於 NVIDIA；而在晶圓代工領域，台積電也大幅領先。
4.  **歷史的錯誤決策：** Intel 曾是 ASML 在 EUV 技術上的早期投資者，但後來因策略失誤而撤資，錯失了打破今日壟斷局面的機會。
5.  **AI 熱潮火上澆油：** 人工智慧的爆發性成長，導致對高階晶片的需求急劇增加，甚至連製造晶片的矽晶圓都出現短缺，進一步推高了整個產業鏈的成本。

## 精彩時刻
- 開場短劇生動地呈現了「NVIDIA -> 台積電 -> ASML」這條令人絕望的價格鏈。
- 諷刺消費者對 NVIDIA 的態度：「還沒氣到去買一張 Radeon 卡，但夠氣到在網路上抱怨。」
- EUV 技術的驚人解釋：在真空中用高功率雷射蒸發錫珠，以創造地球上不存在的極紫外光來「印刷」晶片。
- 提到 ASML 的新一代光刻機要價 4 億美元時，影片傳達出的那種無力感。

# Why Your GPU is So Expensive

## TL;DR
The reason computers (especially GPUs) are getting more expensive is a chain of monopolies and de-facto monopolies in the semiconductor supply chain. From Nvidia's market dominance, to TSMC's cutting-edge fabrication, to ASML's exclusive machinery, a lack of competition at every layer means consumers ultimately foot the bill.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning:** The video starts with a dramatic skit, pointing directly at an Nvidia GPU and its high price. It then reveals that Nvidia is a "fabless" company, meaning it doesn't manufacture its own chips but relies on Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC).
- **Middle:** The narrative then peels back another layer, showing that TSMC's dominance comes from its mastery of cutting-edge chip fabrication, specifically EUV (Extreme Ultraviolet) lithography. However, the machines required for this process are made by only one company in the world: the Dutch firm ASML. A single EUV machine costs $200 million, creating a monopoly within a monopoly.
- **End:** The video concludes that at every critical stage—chip design (Nvidia), manufacturing (TSMC), and equipment (ASML)—the market is dominated by a single player. The AI boom has intensified demand for high-end chips, making the situation worse. While new competition may emerge eventually, high prices are expected to persist for the foreseeable future.

## Key Insights
1.  **A Stack of Monopolies:** The high hardware cost isn't a single company's fault but a structural problem in the industry, composed of Nvidia (near-monopoly in design), TSMC (near-monopoly in fabrication), and ASML (a literal monopoly in equipment).
2.  **ASML is the Ultimate Gatekeeper:** ASML is the only company capable of producing the EUV lithography machines essential for creating advanced chips. Its next-generation machine will cost an astounding $400 million, a price even TSMC is balking at.
3.  **Lack of Competition is the Core Issue:** At every step of the supply chain, there is no significant competitor to apply downward pressure on prices. AMD and Intel are far behind Nvidia in the GPU market, and TSMC is leagues ahead in manufacturing.
4.  **A Costly Historical Blunder:** Intel was an early investor in ASML's EUV development but divested from it due to a strategic misstep, losing a critical opportunity to prevent the current monopoly.
5.  **The AI Boom is Fueling the Fire:** The explosion in AI development has drastically increased demand for high-performance chips, causing shortages even for the raw silicon wafers and driving up costs across the entire ecosystem.

## Notable Moments
- The opening skit vividly illustrating the frustrating price chain from "Nvidia -> TSMC -> ASML."
- The sarcastic observation about consumer anger toward Nvidia: "Not angry enough to buy a Radeon, mind you, but angry enough to complain online."
- The mind-bending explanation of EUV technology: using high-powered lasers to vaporize tin droplets in a complete vacuum to create light that doesn't naturally exist on Earth to "print" chips.
- The sense of helplessness when the video reveals ASML's next-gen machine will cost $400 million.
