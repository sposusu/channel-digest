# 《世紀帝國II》12月2日更新評測：全面分析兵種與文明平衡改動

## 重點摘要
此次更新對多個文明的獨特單位進行了「雙重加成」，使其戰力顯著提升，但也引發了玩法可能單一化的擔憂。實況主對大部分改動持正面態度，但對開發團隊過度強化的平衡策略表示懷疑，同時對新增的紅牛盃賽事地圖感到非常興奮。

## 故事大綱
- **開場**：實況主介紹了《世紀帝國II》於12月2日發布的突擊更新，並宣布新一季的紅牛盃天梯賽即將開始，鼓勵所有玩家參與。他表示將重點評測與線上對戰相關的平衡性改動。
- **中段**：他詳細分析了各項改動。通用方面，雞的食物量增加，鷹勇士費用降低（改回初版價格），而火砲投石機則被調整為「對建築更強、對單位更弱」。在文明方面，他逐一點評了亞美НИ亞、勃根地、西班牙等文明獲得的「雙重加成」(double buff)，認為這些強化有些過頭，可能導致文明玩法僵化。他也提到了契丹和越南（被他稱為"Shu"）等文明受到的削弱。
- **結尾**：實況主主總結認為這次更新利大於弊，但對開發團隊「雙重加成」的平衡策略表示擔憂，認為這可能讓文明失去戰術多樣性。他對新增的九張紅牛盃比賽地圖感到非常期待，並預告將會為這些地圖製作新影片。

## 關鍵見解
1.  **「雙重加成」的平衡策略**：開發團隊傾向於對同一單位或科技進行多重加成，這讓一些本已不弱的單位（如佛萊明民兵、精銳征服者）變得極其強大。
2.  **玩法單一化的風險**：過度強化文明的獨特之處，可能導致玩法僵化，玩家被迫選擇該文明最強勢的單一策略，否則就難以取勝。
3.  **火砲投石機的重新定位**：該單位被明確修改，以削弱其在後期戰役中對單位集群的毀滅性能力，並強化其作為攻城武器的本質。
4.  **舊版強勢單位的回歸**：鷹勇士的費用改回遊戲初版時的設定，預示著中美洲文明的前中期「全軍出擊」(all-in) 戰術將再次崛起。
5.  **對「雙重加成」的質疑**：實況主多次質疑為何要強化一個已經很強的單位，認為這種改動不僅非必要，甚至可能破壞平衡。

## 精彩時刻
- **保加利亞又被「搞弱智了」**：他嘲諷保加利亞的新改動（從城堡能訓練爆破兵）「一如既往地弱智」(petarded)，對該文明幾乎沒有實質幫助。
- **對開發者的靈魂拷問**：在評論幾個他認為不必要的強化時，他反問：「是我錯了，還是他們（開發團隊）瘋了？」(Am I wrong, or they've just lost their minds?)
- **波斯射手可能「壞掉」**：他認為波斯的新科技讓弩兵僅需50木材的改動，非常強力，甚至可能「破壞平衡」(broken)。
- **一眼看穿地圖作者**：看到新地圖的預覽圖，他立刻斷言「這100%是紅牛盃的地圖」，並表示「我一眼就能認出 Cini（地圖製作者）的設計」。

# Age of Empires II December 2nd Update Review: Full Breakdown of Unit & Civ Balance Changes

## TL;DR
The update introduces significant, and sometimes excessive, "double buffs" to the unique aspects of many civilizations, making them much stronger but potentially one-dimensional. While the streamer is mostly positive, he expresses concern over this balancing philosophy and the risk of creating overly rigid strategies.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning**: The streamer introduces a surprise December 2nd update for Age of Empires II and announces the upcoming Red Bull 1v1 ladder season, encouraging everyone to play. He states his review will focus on the competitive balance changes.
- **Middle**: He breaks down the changes in detail. General changes include a buff to chickens and Eagle Warriors (reverting a past nerf), and a rework of the Traction Trebuchet to be stronger against buildings but weaker vs. units. For specific civs, he critiques the "double buffs" given to Armenians, Burgundians, and Spanish, feeling they are unnecessary and make the units too powerful. He also covers nerfs to the Kitans and Vietnamese (which he misidentifies as "Shu").
- **End**: The creator concludes the patch is mostly positive but worries about the "double buffing" trend making civilizations "one-trick ponies." He ends by expressing immense excitement for the nine new Red Bull tournament maps that were added to the game.

## Key Insights
1.  **The "Double Buff" Philosophy**: The development team shows a tendency to apply multiple buffs to the same unit or technology (e.g., Flemish Militia, Conquistador), making them exceptionally powerful instead of making incremental changes.
2.  **Risk of One-Dimensional Civs**: Over-buffing a civilization's unique strengths can stifle strategic diversity, forcing players into a single, dominant strategy or risk losing.
3.  **Repurposing the Traction Trebuchet**: The unit was specifically altered to nerf its anti-unit "deathball" effectiveness in late-game scenarios and refocus it on its primary role as a building destroyer.
4.  **The Return of an Old Meta**: The Eagle Warrior cost reduction reverts them to their original, powerful state from the game's launch, signaling a likely resurgence of aggressive all-in strategies for Mesoamerican civilizations.
5.  **Questioning Unnecessary Buffs**: The streamer repeatedly questioned the logic of buffing units and civs that were already considered strong, suggesting the changes were not needed.

## Notable Moments
- **Bulgarians Get "Petarded"**: He mockingly calls the new Bulgarian change (training Petards from the Krepost) "petarded," stating it's another useless and random buff for the civilization.
- **Questioning the Devs' Sanity**: When reviewing several buffs he deemed unnecessary, he asks, "Am I wrong, or have they just lost their minds?"
- **"Broken" Persians**: He predicts the Persian technology making Arbalesters cost only 50 wood could be meta-defining or even "broken."
- **Spotting the Designer**: Upon seeing the new maps, he instantly identifies them as the Red Bull tournament maps, saying, "I can see a Cini [the map designer] design from a mile away."
