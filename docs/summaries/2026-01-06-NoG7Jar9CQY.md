# 世紀帝國2最獨特的建築：葡萄牙城堡 (Feteria)

## 重點摘要
葡萄牙的「城堡」(Feteria) 是《世紀帝國2》中一個獨特且昂貴的帝王時代建築，它會佔用20個人口空間來無限產生資源。雖然單看人口效率不如20個村民，但其真正的威力在於能快速建立後期經濟，提供源源不絕的黃金與石頭，從而實現對手難以應對的強大黃金單位或防禦戰術。

## 故事大綱
- **開場**：影片介紹了葡萄牙文明獨有的建築「城堡」(Feteria)，強調其獨特性，許多休閒或老玩家甚至可能不認識它。它需要350黃金、300石頭，並且是遊戲中唯一會佔用20個人口空間的建築。
- **中段**：分析「城堡」的成本與效益。一座城堡的資源產出約等於10個村民，雖然看似不划算，但其優勢在於「快速部署」。相較於建立傳統經濟（蓋城鎮中心、生產村民、升級科技），直接蓋城堡能更快地獲得穩定且無需維護的資源流，尤其是黃金和石頭等稀缺資源。
- **結尾**：探討「城堡」衍生的實用戰術。例如，快速升上帝王時代後，利用幾座城堡的資源快速產出火槍兵、手推炮等黃金海淹沒對手。或是結合城堡時代的城堡前置壓迫，然後在後方安全地蓋起城堡轉型。講者分享了自己用此戰術贏得比賽的經驗，證明其在特定情境下的強大威力。

## 關鍵見解
1. **人口是最大成本**：「城堡」最核心的代價不是資源，而是它佔用的20個人口，這使得玩家必須在軍隊數量與經濟潛力之間做出權衡。
2. **速度就是優勢**：它最大的戰略價值在於能跳過繁瑣的經濟發展過程，快速形成一個穩定的後期資源收入，讓玩家能更快地部署帝王時代的強力組合。
3. **無限的稀缺資源**：在地圖資源耗盡的後期，「城堡」能提供無限的黃金和石頭，讓葡萄牙能持續產出火砲塔、巨型投石機或精銳黃金單位，徹底耗盡對手的資源。
4. **完美的文明加成**：「城堡」的黃金產出與葡萄牙所有黃金單位八折的文明加成完美結合，讓其黃金部隊的CP值極高。
5. **攻守兼備的轉型**：一個強大的策略是先在城堡時代發動一波攻勢（例如前置城堡搭配管砲），迫使對手防守，同時在後方安全地蓋起「城堡」，為帝王時代的總攻擊鋪路。

## 精彩時刻
- 影片中提到一個有趣的技巧：先將人口擴張到199/200，然後蓋下第10座「城堡」，人口會變成219/200，但這種極限操作並不實用。
- 講者分享他個人的「零軍事黑森林強攻」戰術，只靠8座城堡與40個村民的經濟，在黑森林地圖上蓋出無限的火砲塔來推進。
- 講者開玩笑地吹噓自己用「城堡」戰術在T90泰坦聯賽中擁有「100%的勝率」，因為他只用過一次而且贏了。

# The Feteria: Age of Empires 2's Most Unique Building

## TL;DR
The Portuguese Feteria is a unique and expensive Imperial Age building that costs 20 population space to generate infinite resources. While less pop-efficient than 20 villagers, its true power lies in rapidly establishing a late-game economy, providing a steady stream of gold and stone to enable powerful gold-heavy armies or defensive strategies that opponents struggle to counter.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning**: The video introduces the Feteria as a one-of-a-kind building exclusive to the Portuguese civilization, noting that many casual or original players might not even recognize it. It costs 350 gold, 300 stone, and uniquely occupies 20 population space.
- **Middle**: The analysis shifts to the Feteria's cost versus benefits. One Feteria generates resources equivalent to about 10 villagers. While this seems inefficient, its advantage is "speed." Compared to building a traditional economy (TCs, villagers, upgrades), simply building Feterias provides a faster path to a stable, maintenance-free income, especially for scarce resources like gold and stone.
- **End**: The video explores practical strategies enabled by the Feteria. For instance, a fast Imperial Age followed by several Feterias can fund a powerful army of Hand Cannoneers or Organ Guns to overwhelm an opponent. Another approach is to combine a Castle Age push (like a castle drop) with a transition into Feterias behind it. The narrator shares his own experiences winning games with this strategy, proving its power in the right context.

## Key Insights
1.  **Population is the Real Cost**: The Feteria's primary cost isn't resources, but the 20 population space it occupies, forcing a strategic trade-off between army size and economic potential.
2.  **Speed is the Advantage**: Its greatest strategic value is its ability to bypass the lengthy process of economic development, allowing a player to quickly establish a stable late-game income and deploy powerful Imperial Age compositions faster.
3.  **Infinite Scarce Resources**: In the late game when map resources are depleted, the Feteria provides an endless supply of gold and stone, allowing the Portuguese to outlast opponents by continually producing Bombard Towers, Trebuchets, or elite gold units.
4.  **Perfect Synergy with Civ Bonuses**: The Feteria's gold generation perfectly complements the Portuguese 20% gold discount on all units, making their gold-based armies incredibly cost-effective.
5.  **A Versatile Offensive Transition**: A powerful strategy involves launching a Castle Age attack (e.g., a forward castle with Organ Guns) to pressure the opponent, while safely building Feterias at home to prepare for a decisive Imperial Age assault.

## Notable Moments
- A "cool trick" is mentioned where a player at 199/200 pop can build a 10th Feteria to reach 219/200 population, although this is impractical.
- The narrator shares his personal "zero military dominant Black Forest push," where he used the economy from 8 Feterias and 40 villagers to spam infinite Bombard Towers across the map.
- He jokingly brags about his "100% success rate in tournaments" with the Feteria strategy, as he has only used it once and won.
