# iPhone 17 評測：一個安卓用戶的掙扎與抉擇

## 重點摘要
評測者在深入體驗 iPhone Air、iPhone 17 及 17 Pro Max 後，雖然對標準版 iPhone 17 的硬體設計與均衡表現給予高度評價，但最終因無法忍受 iOS 在鍵盤自訂性、軟體封閉性及出乎意料的錯誤等核心問題，決定繼續留在安卓陣營。

## 故事大綱
- **開場**：身為一名安卓長期用戶，評測者每年都期望新 iPhone 能說服他轉換平台。他首先體驗了號稱超薄的 iPhone Air，但從一開始就因「移轉到 iOS」工具的失敗而感到挫折。
- **中段**：他發現 iPhone Air 設計雖驚艷，但在人體工學（壓迫小指）、重量平衡和電池續航力方面存在明顯缺陷，感覺像是一款利基市場的初代產品。相比之下，他對標準版 iPhone 17 極為讚賞，稱其為「近年來最均衡的 iPhone」，並盛讚全系列標配的 120Hz ProMotion 螢幕和極其堅固的「陶瓷晶盾 2」玻璃。
- **結尾**：儘管 iPhone 17 硬體出色，但 iOS 26 的封閉生態（無法側載）、糟糕的鍵盤體驗和反常的不穩定表現，成為他無法跨越的障礙。他總結，既然要忍受跟安卓一樣多的軟體錯誤，那不如選擇更開放的系統，最終決定回歸安卓。

## 關鍵見解
1.  **iPhone 17 是最佳選擇**：標準版 iPhone 17 是本代最值得推薦的型號，在功能、表現與價格間取得了最佳平衡，遠勝過妥協的 Air 或對多數人而言過剩的 Pro 機型。
2.  **硬體升級有感**：全系列迎來兩大硬體飛躍——極其耐摔的「陶瓷晶盾 2」玻璃，以及終於下放到所有機型的 120Hz ProMotion 高更新率螢幕。
3.  **iPhone Air 華而不實**：iPhone Air 為了追求極致輕薄，犧牲了手感、電池與喇叭效果，感覺像是一款昂貴且不成熟的初代產品。
4.  **iOS 依然是最大痛點**：iOS 系統是勸退的核心原因。缺乏自訂性（尤其是鍵盤）、系統封閉（如限定特定 App 才能捲動截圖），且新版 iOS 26 異常不穩定。
5.  **跨平台轉移是場災難**：蘋果官方的「移轉到 iOS」App 被評為「完全沒用」，讓從安卓轉換平台的過程充滿了阻礙與不便。

## 精彩時刻
- 形容為超薄的 iPhone Air 裝上保護殼，就像**「點了培根inator漢堡卻配零卡可樂」**一樣，失去了原本的意義。
- 在手機摔到水泥地上後，驚訝地發現螢幕毫髮無傷，忍不住大喊：**「玻璃上的刮痕在哪裡，傑瑞？」**
- 諷刺蘋果公司，說他們**「最喜歡把實用功能藏在『輔助功能』選單裡」**。
- 對 iOS 鍵盤體驗的經典抱怨：「a complete ducking lack of meaningful keyboard customization」。

# iPhone 17 Review: An Android User's Perspective

## TL;DR
The reviewer was highly impressed with the hardware of the new iPhone lineup, calling the base iPhone 17 the best-rounded model in years. However, he ultimately decided to switch back to Android due to fundamental frustrations with iOS, including its lack of customization, closed ecosystem, and uncharacteristic bugginess.

## Story Flow
- **Beginning**: The reviewer, a long-time Android user, begins his annual experiment to see if the new iPhones can make him switch. He starts by daily driving the ultra-thin iPhone Air, but the experience gets off to a rocky start with the failure of the "Move to iOS" app.
- **Middle**: He finds the iPhone Air sleek but ergonomically flawed, with poor weight balance and compromised battery life, deeming it a niche, first-gen product. He then switches to the standard iPhone 17 and is thoroughly impressed, praising it as the "best rounded iPhone in years," and celebrating the new 120Hz ProMotion display (now on all models) and the incredibly durable Ceramic Shield 2 glass.
- **End**: Despite loving the iPhone 17's hardware, he concludes that the core limitations of iOS 26—no sideloading, terrible keyboard customization, and surprising instability—are dealbreakers. He reasons that if he has to put up with a buggy experience, he might as well stick with the more open Android, forcing his return.

## Key Insights
1.  **The iPhone 17 is the Sweet Spot**: The standard iPhone 17 is the star of the lineup, offering the best balance of features, performance, and price for most users, making it a better choice than the compromised Air or the overkill Pro models.
2.  **Hardware Upgrades are Significant**: The entire lineup received two major hardware improvements: the incredibly tough Ceramic Shield 2 glass and the long-awaited inclusion of 120Hz ProMotion displays on non-Pro models.
3.  **The iPhone Air is a Flawed Niche Device**: In its pursuit of thinness, the Air sacrifices ergonomics, battery life, and speaker quality, feeling more like an expensive and unrefined first-generation product.
4.  **iOS is Still the Biggest Barrier**: The iOS experience is the core reason for not switching. It suffers from a lack of customization (especially keyboards), a closed ecosystem (e.g., locking scrolling screenshots to specific apps), and uncharacteristic instability in its latest version.
5.  **Cross-Platform Migration is a Disaster**: Apple's official "Move to iOS" tool is described as "utterly useless," making the transition from Android a frustrating and incomplete process.

## Notable Moments
- Describing putting a case on the thin iPhone Air is **"like ordering a Diet Coke with your Baconator"**—it defeats the entire purpose.
- His shock after dropping the phone on concrete and finding the glass unscathed: **"Where are the scratches on the glass, Jerry?"**
- Joking that Apple's **"favorite hiding place for useful features that should be obvious"** is the accessibility settings menu.
- The perennial complaint about iOS autocorrect: a **"complete ducking lack of meaningful keyboard customization."**
